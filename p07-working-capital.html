Question or case data

Orchid Supplies has average inventory $42,000, cost of sales $252,000, average trade receivables $36,000, credit sales $360,000, average trade payables $30,000 and credit purchases $240,000. Current assets are $110,000 including inventory of $42,000. Current liabilities are $55,000.

Case-data check

Read the full source facts before opening the answer. Trace every figure from the case data into the calculation bridge, journal entry, ledger or statement line.

Required

  • Calculate inventory turnover period, receivables settlement period and payables settlement period.
  • Calculate operating cash cycle.
  • Calculate current ratio and acid-test ratio.

Model answer — structured workings

Working capital and cash operating cycle drill

Working-capital calculations

Inventory days

$42,000 / $252,000 × 365 = 60.8 days
Trace source:

Use average inventory from SFP inventory and cost of sales from SPL cost of goods sold from the inventory or trading schedule.

Receivable days

$36,000 / $360,000 × 365 = 36.5 days
Trace source:

Use average trade receivables from SFP trade receivables and credit sales from sales records or SPL sales revenue if all sales are credit.

Payable days

$30,000 / $240,000 × 365 = 45.6 days
Trace source:

Use average trade payables from SFP trade payables and credit purchases from purchase records. If credit purchases are not given, do not invent them.

Operating cash cycle

60.8 + 36.5 − 45.6 = 51.7 days
Trace source:

This combines three separate working-capital timing measures: inventory days + receivable days − payable days. The components are built from SPL or SFP inputs and credit records.

Current ratio

$110,000 / $55,000 = 2.0 : 1
Trace statement inputs:

Current assets divided by current liabilities.

Acid-test ratio

($110,000 − $42,000) / $55,000 = 1.24 : 1
Trace statement inputs:

Quick assets divided by current liabilities. Quick assets exclude inventory.

Interpretation: The business funds about 52 days between paying/holding inventory and collecting from customers. The acid-test ratio removes inventory because it may not convert into cash quickly.
Self-check: Tie every calculation or classification to the accounting purpose. A correct final answer without the route is not enough.

Explanation for working-capital and operating-cycle answer

Timing bridge

Inventory days:
$42,000 / $252,000 × 365 = 60.8 days

Receivable days:
$36,000 / $360,000 × 365 = 36.5 days

Payable days:
$30,000 / $240,000 × 365 = 45.6 days

Cash-cycle bridge

Operating cash cycle:
60.8 + 36.5 − 45.6 = 51.7 days

This estimates how long cash is tied up from buying or holding inventory through collecting from customers, after allowing for supplier credit.

Liquidity bridge

Current ratio:
$110,000 / $55,000 = 2.0 : 1

Acid-test ratio:
($110,000 − $42,000) / $55,000 = 1.24 : 1
