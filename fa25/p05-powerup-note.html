Full case-data restoration

Use every numbered item below before solving. This mock test is an integrated adjustment question; leaving out one condition changes the journal entry and final adjusted figures.

Source itemFull case data retained for learner use
Trial balance extractAccounts receivable $70,000; Allowance for impairment of accounts receivable $6,300 Cr; Bank loan $180,000 Cr; Inventory $182,500; Cost of goods sold $212,000; Prepaid shop rental expense $110,500; Sales $550,200 Cr; Tax payable $11,700 Cr; Cash at bank $46,100.
Additional information (1)The sales amount in the trial balance for the year included $2,800 worth of hiking sticks sent out on consignment to Walk On Trading on 24 April 2024. The accounts executive recorded the transaction as credit sales and also recorded the related cost of goods sold. The cost of these hiking sticks was $1,650. The sticks were still unsold by Walk On Trading as at 30 April 2024.
Additional information (2)A physical inventory count on 30 April 2024 indicated inventory on hand of $166,900, excluding the hiking sticks held on consignment by Walk On Trading.
Additional information (3)Ending inventory at 30 April 2024 included $5,400 of hiking T-shirts with outdated designs. A customer in Indonesia is willing to buy these T-shirts for $2,300 on FCA terms. Estimated shipping cost is $450.
Additional information (4)Shop rental expense is $8,500 per month, payable quarterly in advance on 1 March, 1 June, 1 September and 1 December.
Additional information (5)On 15 January 2024, Power Up issued 50,000 redeemable preference shares at $1.20 each for cash. They are due for redemption in 10 years. The redeemable preference shares are entitled to annual dividends of $0.21 per share, declared at financial year-end. No accounting entry was recorded for the issue or the relevant dividend/finance cost.
Additional information (6)Power Up estimated that 5% of its accounts receivable balance as at 30 April 2024 would be uncollectible. The company uses the allowance method to value accounts receivable at the end of each financial year.
Additional information (7)The bank loan was obtained on 1 February 2022 at an interest rate of 5% per annum. Interest is payable quarterly in arrears on 1 May, 1 August, 1 November and 1 February each year.
Additional information (8)Current-year tax liability is estimated at $18,500 and has not been accrued. The Comptroller of Income Tax confirmed that tax liability for the year ended 30 April 2023 was fully settled. Any prior-year income tax adjustment should be adjusted in the current financial year. Entries for both the prior-year adjustment and current-year tax had not been made.

Full eight-part additional information restored. Do not solve from the trial balance alone.

Required

  • Prepare all necessary adjusting journal entries at 30 Apr 2024.
  • Show calculation bridges for inventory shortage, NRV, prepaid rent, allowance, interest and tax.

Explanation for Power Up integrated adjustment answer

Inventory / consignment bridge

Reverse fake sale:
Dr Sales 2,800 / Cr A/R 2,800

Restore fake COGS removal:
Dr Inventory 1,650 / Cr COGS 1,650

Inventory per books after restoration:
$182,500 + $1,650 = $184,150

Physical inventory owned:
$166,900 + $1,650 = $168,550

Shortage:
$184,150 − $168,550 = $15,600

Other adjustment bridge

NRV = $2,300 − $450 = $1,850
Write-down = $5,400 − $1,850 = $3,550

Prepaid rent required = $8,500
Rent expense = $110,500 − $8,500 = $102,000

Preference proceeds = 50,000 × $1.20 = $60,000
Finance cost = 50,000 × $0.21 × 3.5 / 12 = $3,062.50

Allowance required = 5% × ($70,000 − $2,800) = $3,360
Allowance reduction = $6,300 − $3,360 = $2,940

Bank interest = $180,000 × 5% × 3 / 12 = $2,250
Tax: clear $11,700 prior year; accrue $18,500 current year.

This integrated case must be solved in order: fix ownership/cut-off errors first, then calculate inventory shortage, then apply NRV, accruals, allowance, interest and tax.
