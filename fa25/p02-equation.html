Explanation for accounting-equation transaction analysis

Profit and equity bridge

Net profit:
Revenue $700 + $400 − wages $500 = $600

Equity:
Owner capital $900 + profit $600 − drawings $50 = $1,450

Liabilities:
$100 + $200 − $200 = $100

Asset bridge

Assets = Liabilities + Equity
Assets = $100 + $1,450 = $1,550

Equipment bought for cash and customer collection are asset swaps: one asset increases while another decreases, so total assets do not change. Drawings reduce equity, not profit.

Self-check: new revenue is recorded when earned; collecting an existing receivable is not new revenue.
