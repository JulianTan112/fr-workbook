Explanation for separate balance-day adjustment cases

Due-diligence treatment check added against the relevant financial-accounting principle.

Each adjustment fixes timing under accrual accounting. Prepaids move expired benefits from asset to expense. Unearned revenue moves from liability to revenue only when earned. Accrued expenses and accrued revenues recognise amounts incurred or earned before cash movement.

The journal amount is the current-period amount still missing from the accounts, not automatically the full cash amount. Use a bridge: total amount, period covered, portion used/earned/incurred, amount already recorded, adjustment required.

Due-diligence check: every adjustment should answer two questions: which period does the item belong to, and what is the existing account balance before adjustment?
