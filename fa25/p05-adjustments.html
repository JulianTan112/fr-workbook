Question 1 [Adapted: Exercise 3-14, Wild, Kwok et al, 2022]

Prepare the necessary year-end adjusting entries for each separate case. Narrations are required. Use the available account names from the partial chart of accounts below.

Separate year-end cases

CaseFull source fact to useImmediate issue
aDepreciation on the business' wind turbine equipment for the year is $5,000.Recognise depreciation expense and accumulated depreciation.
bThe Prepaid Insurance account for the solar panels had a $2,000 debit balance at year-end before adjustment. Analysis shows that $600 of unexpired insurance coverage remains at year-end.Only the expired portion is expense: $2,000 − $600.
cThe business received $3,000 cash in advance for sustainability consulting work and had already recorded the cash receipt. As at year-end, one-third of the consulting work had been performed.One-third of the liability has now been earned as revenue.
dAs at year-end, $1,200 in wages expense for organic produce workers has been incurred but not yet paid.Record the expense and the payable.
eFor the current financial year, the business has earned $400 of interest revenue from investments in socially responsible bonds. The interest revenue will only be received two months after the year-end date.Record the receivable and the revenue now because it has been earned.
Source-complete check

This question is not asking for a statement layout or classification task. It asks for narrated year-end adjusting journals using the provided account names. The answer must therefore preserve the account list, each case fact, the calculation for the adjustment amount, and the narration for every entry.

Required

  • Prepare the necessary year-end adjusting entries for cases (a) to (e).
  • Use the partial chart of accounts provided in the question.
  • Include narrations for each adjusting entry.

Self-check: The source asks for narrations, so each journal entry needs an explanatory line. Debit total equals credit total: $9,000 = $9,000. Cash is not used in these adjusting entries because the cash movement either happened earlier or will happen later.

Explanation for separate balance-day adjustment cases

How to read the source facts

The question gives five independent adjustment cases and a restricted account list. The answer should not add extra required tasks. The only required output is the narrated adjusting journal entries.

Number bridge

Case a: depreciation expense is given directly                       $5,000
Case b: expired insurance = $2,000 − $600                             1,400
Case c: consulting revenue earned = $3,000 × 1/3                       1,000
Case d: unpaid wages incurred are given directly                       1,200
Case e: interest revenue earned but uncollected is given directly        400
Total debit adjustments and total credit adjustments                 $9,000

Statement effect

SPL: recognise depreciation, insurance, wages expense and interest/consulting revenue in the correct period.
SFP: reduce prepaid insurance, reduce unearned revenue, recognise wages payable, recognise interest receivable and recognise accumulated depreciation.
