Why the framework prevents random answers

The Conceptual Framework is the reasoning layer behind accounting treatment. It explains why information is useful, why transactions are recognised on an accrual basis, why assets and liabilities must meet definitions, and why presentation should faithfully represent the economic event rather than merely produce a neat journal entry.

Useful for decisions

Relevance asks whether the information could affect a user’s decision. A material allowance, impairment, liability or accrued revenue should not be ignored just because it is inconvenient.

Faithful, not decorative

Faithful representation requires the statement to depict the actual economic event. A consignment is not ordinary sales revenue if the seller has not really completed a sale to the final customer.

Basis before mechanics

Accrual basis and going concern explain why period-end adjustments, depreciation, prepayments and accruals exist before the learner touches the debit and credit columns.

Five framework lenses used in v25

Framework ideaPlain accounting logicUse it when explaining...Wrong answer it prevents
RelevanceInformation is useful if it can affect decisions through predictive value, confirmatory value or materiality.Allowance for impairment, NRV write-downs, material accruals, liabilities and disclosures.Ignoring an adjustment because the cash has not moved yet.
Faithful representationThe accounting should be complete, neutral and free from material error.Consignment sales, bank errors, PPE disposal, redeemable preference shares, inventory count corrections.Recording a neat but economically wrong entry.
Cost constraintInformation should be useful enough to justify the cost of collecting, preparing and verifying it.Level of detail, immaterial items, whether extra disclosure or measurement effort is justified.Adding excessive detail that does not improve the decision.
Accrual basisRecognise income when earned and expenses when incurred, not merely when cash is received or paid.Accrued revenue, accrued salaries, prepaid insurance, unearned revenue, interest accruals.Treating every case as a cash-basis question.
Going concernAssume the entity continues operating unless the facts say otherwise; this supports normal classification and measurement.PPE depreciation, historical cost, current or non-current classification, prepaid benefits.Using liquidation logic or market-sale logic when normal operations continue.

Treatment decision bridge

Use this five-step bridge before writing an explanation. It forces the answer to connect the source facts to the accounting treatment.

StepQuestion to askOutput in the answer
1. Source factWhat exact fact in the question creates the issue?Quote or paraphrase the amount, date, condition or missing entry.
2. ElementWhich element is affected: asset, liability, equity, income or expense?State the account affected and whether it belongs in SPL, SFP or both.
3. Framework lensWhich principle explains the treatment?Use relevance, faithful representation, cost constraint, accrual basis or going concern.
4. CalculationHow is the number derived?Show the calculation bridge before the journal entry.
5. Statement effectWhat changes in the statements?State SPL effect, SFP effect, or cash-flow effect where relevant.

Framework examples linked to v25 practice

Practice situationFramework reasonCalculation or treatment bridgeStatement effect
Renaldo accrued feesAccrual basis: fees earned but not invoiced are still income for the period.Dr Fees Receivable 4,150; Cr Fees Earned 4,150.SPL revenue increases; SFP current asset increases.
Renaldo prepaid insuranceFaithful representation and accrual basis: only the expired portion is expense; unexpired benefit remains an asset.4,500 − 1,900 = 2,600 insurance expense.SPL expense increases; SFP prepaid insurance remains at 1,900.
PPE disposal gainFaithful representation: compare sale proceeds with carrying amount, not original cost.Cost 6,000 − accumulated depreciation 4,750 = carrying amount 1,250; proceeds 1,500 − 1,250 = gain 250.SFP removes PPE carrying amount; SPL reports gain on disposal.
Inventory lower of cost and NRVRelevance and faithful representation: inventory should not be carried above recoverable amount.Compare cost with NRV item by item where required.SFP inventory decreases; SPL expense or cost adjustment increases.
Allowance for impairmentRelevance and faithful representation: receivables should show the amount expected to be collectible.Required allowance is based on adjusted accounts receivable; only the movement is journalised.SFP net receivables decrease; SPL impairment expense adjusts profit.
Bank reconciliationFaithful representation: cash records must distinguish bank timing items from business record errors or omissions.Bank-side items reconcile the bank statement; book-side items are journalised.Adjusted cash balance must agree on both sides.

How to write the explanation in practice answers

Good explanation format:

Source fact → framework reason → calculation bridge → journal or ledger effect → statement effect.

Weak answerBetter answer
“Record accrued revenue because it is accrued revenue.”“The fees were earned before year-end but not invoiced. Under accrual basis, revenue is recognised when earned, so Dr Fees Receivable and Cr Fees Earned. This increases current assets and SPL revenue.”
“Record depreciation because PPE depreciates.”“The business is assumed to continue operating, so PPE cost is allocated over useful life. Depreciation recognises the portion of asset service potential consumed this period.”
“Recognise a gain because cash is received.”“This is a PPE disposal. Compare sale proceeds with carrying amount. Proceeds exceed carrying amount, so the difference is a gain, not sales revenue.”

Common errors this page is designed to stop

  • Using cash movement as the only trigger when the question is testing accrual accounting.
  • Calling every receipt “revenue” even when it is unearned revenue, loan proceeds or share issue proceeds.
  • Calling PPE sale proceeds ordinary sales revenue instead of separating disposal gain or loss from investing cash flow.
  • Using “matching” to invent an asset that does not meet the asset definition.
  • Adding unnecessary disclosure or classifications without explaining relevance or cost constraint.
