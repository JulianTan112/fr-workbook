FIFO explanation: sales remove oldest cost layers first; after 22 Jun the remaining layer is 11 units at $42. The 30 Jun purchase creates a new layer at $43.
WAC explanation: average cost is recalculated only after purchases in a perpetual system, then used for subsequent sales until the next purchase.
Self-check: Under FIFO, ending inventory is $806. Under WAC, ending inventory is approximately $802.33; small rounding differences are acceptable.

Explanation for FIFO or WAC stock ledger answer

FIFO bridge

6 Jun sale: 16 × $41 = $656
18 Jun sale: 9 × $41 + 3 × $42 = $369 + $126 = $495
22 Jun sale: 4 × $42 = $168
FIFO Cost of goods sold = $656 + $495 + $168 = $1,319

FIFO ending inventory

Remaining after sales: 11 units × $42 = $462
30 Jun purchase:       8 units × $43 = $344
FIFO ending inventory: $462 + $344 = $806

WAC bridge

After 13 Jun purchase:
($369 + $756) / 27 units = $41.6667 per unit

18 Jun sale: 12 × $41.6667 = $500.00
22 Jun sale: 4 × $41.6667 = $166.67
WAC Cost of goods sold = $656 + $500.00 + $166.67 = $1,322.67
WAC ending inventory = $458.33 + $344 = $802.33

In this rising-cost example, FIFO leaves newer higher-cost units in inventory, so FIFO ending inventory is higher than WAC and FIFO Cost of goods sold is lower than WAC.
