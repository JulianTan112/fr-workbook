Explanation for liability classification answer

Due-diligence treatment check added against the relevant financial-accounting principle.

Classification depends on settlement timing and the nature of the obligation. Current liabilities are expected to be settled within the operating cycle or within twelve months. Non-current liabilities are longer-term unless due within the current classification window.

Some items sound like equity but behave like liabilities when redemption or fixed payment obligations exist. This is why preference shares, loans, bonds, tax obligations and dividends payable must be read according to their terms.

Due-diligence check: first identify whether an obligation exists, then decide current/non-current based on expected settlement timing.
