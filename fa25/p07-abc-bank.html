Question 2

The cash account for ABC Learning Centre at August 1, 20X1 indicated a debit balance of $2,705.37. During August, the total cash receipt was $21,077.75 and cheques payment totaled $20,453.25. The bank statement indicated a credit balance of $3,465.50 on August 31, 20X1.

The records revealed the following differences:

ItemOriginal source fact
aCheques that creditors have yet to bank in totaled $2,003.84.
bA deposit of $1,148.21, representing receipts of August 31, had been deposited in the bank account only on September 1.
cA cheque payment for $110 had been recorded by the bank as $1,100.
dA deposit of $1,250 was recorded by the bank as $1,520.
Source-trace note

The source question only asks for the bank reconciliation statement. Because the instruction says business records are correct, the two recording errors are bank errors, not book-side journal entries.

Required

  • Prepare a bank reconciliation statement as at 31 August 20X1.
  • If there are any errors, assume that the business’ records are correct.

Explanation for ABC Learning Centre bank reconciliation

Bank-side calculation: start with the bank statement balance $3,465.50 Cr. Add the deposit-in-transit $1,148.21 because the business recorded it but the bank had not yet credited it by 31 August. Add $990.00 because the bank wrongly treated a $110 cheque payment as $1,100, so the bank reduced the balance by $990 too much. Deduct the bank deposit error of $270.00 because the bank recorded a $1,250 deposit as $1,520. Deduct unpresented cheques of $2,003.84 because the business recorded the payment but the creditors had not yet banked the cheques.

$3,465.50 + $1,148.21 + $990.00 − $270.00 − $2,003.84 = $3,329.87

Cash book bridge: the cash book balance is derived from the source facts: opening debit balance $2,705.37 + August cash receipts $21,077.75 − cheques paid $20,453.25 = $3,329.87.

$2,705.37 + $21,077.75 − $20,453.25 = $3,329.87

Journal logic: no book-side journal entry is required here because the source says business records are correct. The differences are timing items or bank errors, so the reconciliation adjusts the bank side only.
