Explanation for ABC bank reconciliation

Due-diligence treatment check added against the relevant financial-accounting principle.

Bank reconciliation separates timing differences, bank errors and book-side omissions/errors. Deposits in transit and unpresented cheques are bank-side timing items because the business has recorded them but the bank has not.

Book-side items require journal entries because the business records are missing or wrong. Bank-side timing items do not require business journal entries because the cash book already contains them.

Due-diligence check: for every reconciling item, ask “which side has not recorded this yet?” before deciding bank side, book side, or journal entry.
