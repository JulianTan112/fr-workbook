🛒 Accounting for Trading Businesses

1. Service vs trading businesses

Service businesses earn revenue by providing services. Trading or merchandising businesses earn profit by buying goods for resale and selling them to customers. The major difference in performance reporting is that trading businesses show cost of goods sold and gross profit.

Profit computation comparison
Business typeRevenue lineCost structureProfit presentation
Service businessService revenueOperating expensesService revenue − operating expenses = net profit.
Trading businessSales revenueCost of goods sold plus operating expensesSales − COGS = gross profit; gross profit − operating expenses = net profit.

4. Returns and allowances

Returns and allowances
EventBuyer / seller actionJournal patternInventory impact
Purchase returnBuyer returns goods to supplier before payment.Dr Accounts Payable; Cr Inventory.Inventory decreases because the buyer no longer holds those goods.
Purchase allowanceSupplier gives price reduction for defective/unacceptable goods kept by buyer.Dr Accounts Payable; Cr Inventory.Inventory cost is reduced.
Sales return — goods in good conditionCustomer returns goods and seller accepts return.Dr Sales Returns; Cr Accounts Receivable/Cash. Also Dr Inventory; Cr COGS.Inventory is restored at cost.
Sales allowanceCustomer keeps goods but receives price reduction.Dr Sales Allowances; Cr Accounts Receivable/Cash.No inventory entry because goods are not returned.
Sales return — damaged goodsCustomer returns goods but they cannot be restored to saleable inventory.Revenue reversal still required; inventory entry depends on NRV/scrap value.Do not blindly restore inventory at full cost if damaged.

5. Transportation costs and goods-in-transit cut-off

Freight terms determine when ownership and risk transfer, which determines whether goods in transit are included in the buyer’s inventory at year-end.

Freight and ownership cut-off
Term used in courseWhen buyer includes goodsTypical treatment
FCA named place / origin-style transferWhen seller delivers goods to carrier at the named place.Buyer owns goods while in transit; freight-in may be included in inventory cost if necessary to bring inventory to sale location.
CPT named destination / destination-style transferWhen goods arrive at destination.Buyer excludes goods before arrival; seller retains inventory until delivery point.
Goods in transit — FCA example

Exam trap: Physical location is not enough. The question tests ownership transfer. Goods can be on a ship and still belong to the buyer if title/risk has passed.

6. Trade discounts and cash discounts

Discount logic
Discount typeMeaningAccounting treatment
Trade discountPrice reduction given before the sale is recorded, often from list price.Record sale/purchase at the discounted invoice price only; no separate discount account.
Sales / settlement discountReduction offered for early payment by customer.Adjust receivable/cash according to terms used in question; reduces amount collected from customer.
Purchase / settlement discountReduction received for early payment to supplier.Reduces amount paid and may reduce inventory cost or be recorded according to course instructions.

8. Trading-business statement presentation

Trading-business SPL extract
LineComputation
Sales revenueGross sales less sales returns and allowances and sales discounts if applicable.
Cost of goods soldInventory cost transferred to expense when goods are sold, adjusted for shortages/surpluses.
Gross profitNet sales − COGS.
Operating expensesSelling and administrative expenses, such as freight-out if seller pays delivery to customer.
Net profitGross profit − operating expenses.
