Question or case data

ItemAmount or rate
Cost of machinery$80,000
Accumulated depreciation$10,000
Sales proceeds excluding GST$75,000
GST rate9%

The company is GST-registered and disposes of the machine for cash or credit to a local buyer.

Required

  1. Calculate the carrying amount of the machine.
  2. Calculate the gain or loss on disposal before GST.
  3. Calculate the GST payable or output tax.
  4. Prepare the General Journal entry.
  5. Show ledger closure for Machinery and Accumulated Depreciation.

4. Explanation for the common confusion

The 9% is not multiplied twice. The Cash or Receivables line shows the invoice total: $75,000 × 109% = $81,750. The GST Payable line shows the GST part inside that invoice: $75,000 × 9% = $6,750. The gain uses the GST-exclusive selling price: $75,000 - $70,000 = $5,000.

Explanation for GST disposal answer

Carrying amount bridge

Machinery cost                         $80,000
Less: Accumulated depreciation         (10,000)
Carrying amount                        $70,000

Invoice or GST bridge

Selling price before GST               $75,000
Output GST: $75,000 × 9%                 6,750
Invoice total / Cash or Accounts Receivable             $81,750

Gain bridge

GST-exclusive selling price            $75,000
Less: carrying amount                  (70,000)
Gain on disposal                         $5,000

GST is not part of the disposal gain. Output GST is a tax liability collected on behalf of the tax authority. The gain compares GST-exclusive proceeds with carrying amount.

Cash-flow classification bridge

Investing cash inflow from PPE disposal:  $75,000 selling price before GST
Output GST liability:                       $6,750
Cash or receivable collected:              $81,750

For cash-flow activity classification, the PPE disposal proceeds belong in investing activities. GST collected is a tax-control liability movement, not part of the disposal gain. See PPE disposal cash-flow bridge.
