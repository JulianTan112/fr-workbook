Question or case data

A $1,000 receivable exists at 31 Dec 2024. The business expects the receivable may be uncollectible at 31 Dec 2024. The customer is confirmed bad and written off on 31 Dec 2025. The customer unexpectedly pays on 26 May 2026.

Compare the accounting under allowance method and direct write-off method.

Explanation for allowance vs direct write-off timeline

Allowance method bridge

31 Dec 2024 expected loss:
Dr Impairment Loss                         1,000
    Cr Allowance for Impairment                   1,000

31 Dec 2025 write-off:
Dr Allowance for Impairment                1,000
    Cr Accounts Receivable                         1,000

The write-off uses the allowance already created, so no fresh impairment expense is recorded on the write-off date.

Recovery bridge

Reinstate receivable:
Dr Accounts Receivable                     1,000
    Cr Allowance for Impairment                   1,000

Collect cash:
Dr Cash                                    1,000
    Cr Accounts Receivable                         1,000

Direct write-off bridge

Under direct write-off, no 2024 expected-loss entry is made. Expense appears only when the debt is confirmed bad, which changes profit timing compared with the allowance method.
