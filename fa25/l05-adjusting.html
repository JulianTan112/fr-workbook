🛠️ Balance Day Adjustments / Adjusting Entries

Accrual accounting at period-end: adjust accounts so statements are truthful for the reporting period.

1. Accounting period and accrual basis

Useful information must reach decision makers frequently. The accounting period principle divides business activity into periods such as months, quarters or years. Adjusting entries ensure revenue and expenses belong to the correct period.

Cash basis vs accrual basis
BasisWhen transactions are recordedExample
Cash basisOnly when cash is received or paid.Cash received on 18 Jan for goods delivered on 5 Jan: record revenue on 18 Jan.
Accrual basisRevenue when goods/services are delivered; expenses when incurred.Goods delivered on 5 Jan on credit: Dr Accounts Receivable; Cr Sales on 5 Jan, then Dr Cash; Cr Accounts Receivable when collected.

2. Why adjusting entries are needed

Adjusting entries are made at the end of an accounting period. They usually do not involve cash at the adjustment date. They ensure the Statement of Profit or Loss shows all revenues and expenses applicable to the period and the Statement of Financial Position shows outstanding assets and liabilities at period end.

Adjustment error effects
If not adjustedSPL effectSFP effect
Accrued revenue omittedRevenue and profit understated.Asset / receivable understated.
Accrued expense omittedExpense understated and profit overstated.Liability understated.
Prepaid expense not adjustedExpense understated.Asset overstated.
Unearned revenue not adjustedRevenue understated if earned portion not transferred.Liability overstated.
Depreciation omittedExpense understated and profit overstated.Asset carrying amount overstated; accumulated depreciation understated.

AdjustmentHow to calculateJournal logic

SuppliesExpense = supplies account balance − physical supplies on hand.Dr Supplies Expense; Cr Supplies.

5. Period cut-off illustration: rent spanning two months

If monthly rent covers 15 April to 14 May, April’s financial statements should include only the April portion. The remaining portion is a prepaid asset at 30 April because it relates to May.

Rent cut-off analysis
Period coveredAccounting treatment
15 Apr–30 AprRecognise as rent expense in April.
1 May–14 MayKeep as prepaid rent at 30 April.

6. Adjusted trial balance and financial statements

Unadjusted trial balanceAccount balances before year-end adjustments.
Adjusting entriesRecord accruals, deferrals, depreciation and estimates.
Post adjustmentsUpdate each affected ledger account.
Adjusted trial balanceList balances after all adjustments.
Financial statementsPrepare SPL, SCE and SFP from adjusted balances.
Self-check: Do not prepare statements from the unadjusted trial balance when additional year-end information is provided.
