Framework users and assumptions drill

Framework classification table

CaseClassificationWhy it matters
ALender or creditor decisionLenders focus on repayment capacity: liquidity, solvency and cash-generation evidence.
BCost constraintUseful information is not costless; benefits should justify preparation and verification costs.
CAccrual basisRevenue is recognised when earned, not merely when cash is collected.
DGoing concernAssets can be used and depreciated over useful life because the entity is expected to continue.
ESupplier or trade creditor decisionTrade creditors use accounting information to judge payment risk and credit terms.
Self-check: Tie every calculation or classification to the accounting purpose. A correct final answer without the route is not enough.

Explanation for users, assumptions and reporting boundary

Case-fact bridge

A lender renews a bank loan → Creditor or lender decision Disclosure cost exceeds likely benefit → Cost constraint Revenue recorded when service performed → Accrual basis Asset depreciated because business continues → Going concern Supplier checks credit-period payment → Supplier or trade creditor decision

The accounting logic is to identify what kind of decision or assumption the case is testing before writing the explanation. Accrual basis uses earning or incurrence rather than cash timing. Going concern justifies using or depreciating assets over future periods.

Reporting boundaryBusiness records belong to the reporting entity, not the owner personally.
Decision usefulnessLenders and suppliers focus on repayment ability, liquidity and solvency.

Self-check: ask “Who is deciding?”, “Which assumption supports the treatment?”, and “Which constraint limits disclosure?”
