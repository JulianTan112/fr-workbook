Explanation for users, assumptions and reporting boundary

Due-diligence treatment check added against the relevant financial-accounting principle.

General-purpose financial reports are prepared for decision users, especially investors, lenders and other creditors. Other users such as suppliers, employees and tax authorities may also read statements, but the main framework logic is decision-usefulness.

The assumptions create the reporting boundary. Business entity separates the business from its owners. Going concern supports normal asset/liability classification and measurement. Accrual accounting records economic activity when earned or incurred, not merely when cash is received or paid.

Due-diligence check: when the case mentions an owner paying privately, a long-term business plan, or revenue earned before cash, identify the assumption before deciding treatment.
