Explanation for Statement of Changes in Equity answer

Dividend bridge

Ordinary dividend declared:
40,000 shares × $2 = $80,000

Preference dividend declared:
2,000 shares × $3 = $6,000

Total dividends declared:
$80,000 + $6,000 = $86,000

Retained earnings bridge

Closing retained earnings:
Opening $260,000 + profit $178,000 − ordinary dividend $80,000 − preference dividend $6,000 = $352,000

SOCIE bridge

Total equity:
Ordinary shares $800,000 + preference shares $50,000 + retained earnings $352,000 = $1,202,000

Dividend declaration reduces retained earnings. Later dividend payment clears Dividends Payable and does not reduce retained earnings again.

Cash-flow classification bridge

Dividend declaration alone is not a cash-flow payment. When the dividend is actually paid in cash, the common workbook treatment classifies it as a financing activity because cash is distributed to owners.
