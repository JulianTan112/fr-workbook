Additional information

  • Sales included $2,800 of hiking sticks sent on consignment to Walk On Trading on 24 April 2024. The transaction was recorded as a normal sale on credit and Cost of goods sold was recorded. Cost of those goods: $1,650. The goods remained unsold at 30 April 2024.
  • Physical inventory count on 30 April 2024 showed $166,900 on hand, excluding the consignment goods held by Walk On Trading.
  • Ending inventory included $5,400 of hiking T-shirts with outdated designs. They can be sold in Indonesia for $2,300 on FCA terms; estimated shipping cost is $450.
  • Shop rental expense at $8,500 per month is payable quarterly in advance on 1 March, 1 June, 1 September, and 1 December.
  • On 15 January 2024, Power Up issued 50,000 redeemable preference shares at $1.20 each, redeemable in 10 years. Annual dividend entitlement: $0.21 per share, declared at financial year-end. No entry has been recorded.
  • Allowance for impairment should be 5% of accounts receivable at 30 April 2024 using the allowance method.
  • Bank loan was obtained on 1 February 2022 at 5% per annum. Interest is payable quarterly in arrears on 1 May, 1 August, 1 November, and 1 February.
  • Current-year tax liability was estimated at $18,500 and not accrued. Prior-year tax payable had been fully settled, but no adjustment had been made.

Allowance adjustment logic: after reversing the incorrect consignment sale, gross Accounts Receivable is $70,000 − $2,800 = $67,200. Power Up estimates 5% will be uncollectible, so the required allowance is $67,200 × 5% = $3,360.
Why debit Allowance? Allowance for impairment is a contra-asset with a normal credit balance. The existing allowance is $6,300 credit, but the required ending allowance is only $3,360 credit. Therefore the allowance is too high by $6,300 − $3,360 = $2,940. Debit Allowance by $2,940 to reduce the contra-asset.
Gross vs net receivables: this entry does not increase gross Accounts Receivable. Gross Accounts Receivable stays at $67,200. What increases is the net carrying amount of receivables, because the contra-asset deduction becomes smaller.

The consigned hiking sticks still belong to Power Up because Walk On had not sold them by year-end. Reverse the fake sale and restore the consignment inventory first. For the shortage entry, follow the source-answer bridge: the $1,650 consignment cost is handled in the separate reversal entry and is therefore deducted from the shortage calculation, giving $13,950.
