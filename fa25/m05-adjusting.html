Additional information

  • Sales included $2,800 of hiking sticks sent on consignment to Walk On Trading on 24 April 2024. The transaction was recorded as a normal sale on credit and COGS was recorded. Cost of those goods: $1,650. The goods remained unsold at 30 April 2024.
  • Physical inventory count on 30 April 2024 showed $16,900 on hand, excluding the consignment goods held by Walk On Trading.
  • Ending inventory included $5,400 of hiking T-shirts with outdated designs. They can be sold in Indonesia for $2,300 on FCA terms; estimated shipping cost is $450.
  • Shop rental expense at $8,500 per month is payable quarterly in advance on 1 March, 1 June, 1 September, and 1 December.
  • On 15 January 2024, Power Up issued 50,000 redeemable preference shares at $1.20 each, redeemable in 10 years. Annual dividend entitlement: $0.21 per share, declared at financial year-end. No entry has been recorded.
  • Allowance for impairment should be 5% of accounts receivable at 30 April 2024 using the allowance method.
  • Bank loan was obtained on 1 February 2022 at 5% per annum. Interest is payable quarterly in arrears on 1 May, 1 August, 1 November, and 1 February.
  • Current-year tax liability was estimated at $18,500 and not accrued. Prior-year tax payable had been fully settled, but no adjustment had been made.

Explanation for Power Up mock test answer

Due-diligence treatment check added against the relevant financial-accounting principle.

This mock requires full additional-information reading before journalising. Consignment, NRV, rent, redeemable preference shares, allowance, interest and tax all affect the trial balance in different ways.

Because the preference shares are redeemable, the return is treated as finance cost over the period outstanding, not a full-year ordinary dividend. Inventory is adjusted in stages: reverse the consignment error, compare adjusted book inventory with physical count, then recognise any NRV write-down.

Due-diligence check: separate each adjustment, show calculation bridge, post to ledger, and only then prepare statement figures.
