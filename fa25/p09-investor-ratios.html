Question or case data

Maple Ltd reports profit after tax $180,000, preference dividends $20,000, ordinary dividends $64,000, ordinary shares outstanding 400,000, market price per ordinary share $2.40 and ordinary shareholders’ equity $1,000,000.

Case-data check

Read the full source facts before opening the answer. Trace every figure from the case data into the calculation bridge, journal entry, ledger or statement line.

Required

  • Calculate earnings available for ordinary shareholders.
  • Calculate EPS, dividend payout ratio, dividend yield, Price-earnings ratio and book value per share.
  • State why these are interpretation tools rather than journal entries.

Model answer — structured workings

Investor ratio interpretation drill

Investor ratios

Earnings available to ordinary shareholders

$180,000 − $20,000 = $160,000
Trace source:

Start with profit after tax, then deduct preference dividends from the equity or dividend information. This figure feeds EPS and payout calculations.

EPS

$160,000 / 400,000 = $0.40 per share
Trace source:

Profit after tax minus preference dividends from equity or dividend records, divided by weighted-average ordinary shares from the share records. Market price is not used in EPS.

Dividend payout ratio

$64,000 / $160,000 × 100 = 40%
Trace source:

Ordinary dividends from dividend or equity records or earnings available to ordinary shareholders from SPL profit after tax adjusted for preference dividends.

Dividend per ordinary share

$64,000 / 400,000 = $0.16 per share
Trace source:

Use ordinary dividends declared or paid from the equity or dividend information and divide by ordinary shares outstanding from the share records.

Dividend yield

$0.16 / $2.40 × 100 = 6.67%
Trace source:

Dividend per share comes from dividend records; market price per share is outside the financial statements. Do not look for market price inside the SPL or SFP.

Price-earnings ratio

$2.40 / $0.40 = 6 times
Trace source:

Market price per share is outside the financial statements. EPS is calculated from profit after tax, preference dividends and ordinary shares.

Book value per ordinary share

$1,000,000 / 400,000 = $2.50
Trace statement inputs:

Ordinary shareholders’ equity divided by ordinary shares outstanding.

Interpretation: These ratios use profit, dividends, equity and market price to interpret shareholder return. They do not by themselves create journal entries.
Self-check: Tie every calculation or classification to the accounting purpose. A correct final answer without the route is not enough.

Explanation for investor-ratio answer

Earnings bridge

Earnings available to ordinary shareholders:
Profit after tax $180,000 − preference dividends $20,000 = $160,000

EPS:
$160,000 / 400,000 ordinary shares = $0.40 per share

Dividend bridge

Payout ratio:
$64,000 / $160,000 × 100 = 40%

Dividend per share:
$64,000 / 400,000 = $0.16

Dividend yield:
$0.16 / $2.40 × 100 = 6.67%

Market or equity bridge

Price-earnings ratio:
$2.40 / $0.40 = 6 times

Book value per share:
$1,000,000 / 400,000 = $2.50

Market price is external market data, not a line item inside the SPL or SFP. EPS starts from profit after tax after preference dividends because ordinary shareholders receive what remains.
