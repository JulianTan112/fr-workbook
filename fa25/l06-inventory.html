2. Determining inventory items: ownership first

Inventory includes all goods the company owns and holds for sale, regardless of physical location. Special attention is needed for goods in transit, consignment goods, and damaged or obsolete goods.

Inventory inclusion rules
ItemInclude in inventory ofReason
FCA goods in transitBuyer once shipped/delivered to carrier at named place.Ownership/risk has transferred to buyer.
CPT goods in transitBuyer only upon arrival at destination.Seller retains inventory before destination arrival.
Goods on consignmentConsignor.Consignee sells for owner but does not own the goods.
Consignee-held goodsNot consignee inventory.Consignee has possession, not ownership.
Damaged goods that cannot be soldExclude or write off.No future economic benefit as inventory.
Damaged goods that can be soldInclude at NRV.Recoverable amount is sales price less selling costs.

7. Lower of cost and net realisable value

Inventory is measured at the lower of cost and net realisable value (NRV). NRV is estimated selling price less costs necessary to make the sale.

9. Inventory impairment and contra-account language

The lecture notes use impairment language when inventory cannot recover its original cost. In inventory questions, the issue normally appears as obsolescence, damage, or NRV below cost.

Impairment idea

An asset is impaired when its recorded cost is higher than the amount expected to be recovered. For inventory, this is handled through lower of cost and net realisable value.

Direct reduction approach

Dr Inventory Write-down / COGS; Cr Inventory. The inventory account itself is reduced to the lower recoverable amount.

Allowance approach

Dr Inventory Obsolescence / Write-down Expense; Cr Allowance for Inventory Obsolescence. The allowance is a contra-asset deducted against inventory in the SFP.

How to read impairment / contra-account wording
Phrase in questionAccounting meaningAnswer format
NRV is lower than costInventory is impaired / written down.Show cost, NRV, write-down and journal entry.
Allowance for inventory obsolescenceContra-asset account deducted from inventory.Present Inventory less Allowance for Inventory Obsolescence.
Damaged goods can still be soldInclude at NRV, not original cost.Use estimated selling price less selling costs.
Damaged goods cannot be soldNo future economic benefit.Exclude from inventory and recognise loss/write-off.
