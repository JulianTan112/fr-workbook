⚖️ Lesson 01 · Regulatory Environment

Core notes

Financial reporting exists to support resource-provision decisions by investors, lenders and creditors. Useful information must be relevant and faithfully represented, then enhanced by comparability, verifiability, timeliness and understandability. Ethics questions should identify the fundamental principle at risk and one of five threats: self-interest, self-review, advocacy, familiarity or intimidation.

Practice answer route / key pattern

Ethics answer format: Threat → Why it arises → Principle at risk → Safeguard.

🧮 Lesson 02 · Accounting Equation & Financial Statements

Core notes

Assets = Liabilities + Equity. Revenue and expenses affect profit, which affects equity. Drawings reduce equity but are not expenses. Statement of Profit or Loss contains revenue and expenses; Statement of Financial Position contains assets, liabilities and equity.

Practice answer route / key pattern

Practice route: transaction-effect table → ending account balances → SPL → SFP.

📒 Lesson 03 · Recording Business Transactions

Core notes

Use the accounting cycle: source documents → analysis → general journal → general ledger → trial balance → financial statements. AED LRC is the core debit/credit memory aid. Trial balance proves equality of total debits and credits only; it does not prove everything is correct.

Practice answer route / key pattern

Journal format: date, debit account, credit account, amounts, narration. Ledger format: post by account and calculate balance.

🛒 Lesson 04 · Accounting for Trading Businesses

Core notes

Trading businesses sell inventory. Under perpetual inventory, sales require both revenue and cost entries. Freight-in is inventory cost for the buyer; freight-out is selling expense for the seller. Returns, allowances, discounts, shortage and surplus must be treated separately.

Practice answer route / key pattern

Cut-off: FCA origin/handed to carrier can create buyer inventory in transit; CPT destination usually waits until arrival.

🛠️ Lesson 05 · Balance Day Adjustments

Core notes

Adjusting entries align revenue and expenses with the accounting period. Main types: accrued expense, accrued revenue, prepaid expense, unearned revenue and depreciation. Standard adjustments usually do not involve Cash at Bank.

Practice answer route / key pattern

Common entries: Dr Expense Cr Payable; Dr Receivable Cr Revenue; Dr Expense Cr Prepaid; Dr Unearned Revenue Cr Revenue; Dr Depreciation Expense Cr Accumulated Depreciation.

📦 Lesson 06 · Inventories

Core notes

Inventory includes goods owned and held for sale regardless of physical location. Special attention: goods in transit, consignment goods, damaged/obsolete goods. Costing methods include FIFO and weighted average. Ending inventory measured at lower of cost and NRV.

Practice answer route / key pattern

Practice route: stock ledger card → COGS → ending inventory → LCNRV write-down if required.

💵 Lesson 07 · Cash, Receivables & Bank Reconciliation

Core notes

Cash requires internal control: segregation of duties, prompt banking, authorised payments, vouchers and documentation. Bank reconciliation separates bank-side reconciling items from book-side adjusting items. Receivables can be accounted for using direct write-off or allowance method.

Practice answer route / key pattern

Bank side: deposit-in-transit, unpresented cheques, bank errors. Book side: bank charges, EFT receipts, dishonoured cheques, business errors.

🏭 Lesson 08 · Property, Plant & Equipment

Core notes

PPE is tangible non-current asset used in operations. Capitalise purchase price and directly attributable costs needed to bring the asset to working condition. Expense routine repair, training and period insurance. Depreciation allocates cost; accumulated depreciation is contra-asset.

Practice answer route / key pattern

Disposal: remove cost and accumulated depreciation; compare proceeds excluding GST with carrying amount to determine gain/loss.

🏛️ Lesson 09 · Shareholders’ Equity

Core notes

Company is a separate legal entity owned by shareholders. Ordinary shares, preference shares, retained earnings and dividends affect equity. Dividends declared reduce retained earnings and create dividends payable; they are not expenses.

Practice answer route / key pattern

Share issue for non-cash asset: use reliable fair value evidence. SOCIE reconciles equity movements.

📑 Lesson 10 · Current & Non-Current Liabilities

Core notes

Classify liabilities by settlement timing. Corporate expenses include directors’ remuneration and auditors’ remuneration. Income tax is recognised in year profit is earned, with prior-year shortfall/excess adjusted in current year. GST collected is output tax payable; GST paid may be input tax.

Practice answer route / key pattern

Company SPL/SFP templates are examinable placement guides: other income, selling/admin/financial expenses, tax, assets, equity and liabilities.

One-page exam trap checklist

AreaTrap check
Regulatory / EthicsName the threat exactly: self-interest, self-review, advocacy, familiarity or intimidation. Then identify why judgement/objectivity is at risk.
Accounting EquationCollection from a credit customer is not new revenue. It is Dr Cash / Cr Accounts Receivable only.
Recording TransactionsUse AED LRC first. Then journalise, post to ledger, prepare trial balance, then statements.
Trading BusinessUnder perpetual inventory, sales of goods usually need two entries: revenue side and cost side.
FCA / CPTGoods shipped before year-end are not automatically buyer inventory. Check ownership transfer: FCA origin/handed to carrier vs CPT destination/arrival.
Adjusting EntriesStandard balance-day adjustments generally do not use Cash at Bank.
InventoryPhysical possession is not ownership. Consignment goods remain inventory of consignor until third-party sale.
Bank ReconciliationDeposits-in-transit and unpresented cheques are bank-side timing items; bank charges, EFT receipts and dishonoured cheques are book-side journal items.
ReceivablesAllowance write-off does not create a new impairment expense; Dr Allowance / Cr A/R.
PPEAccumulated depreciation is a contra-asset, not a liability.
GST disposalGST is calculated once on selling price excluding GST. Gain is sale proceeds excluding GST less carrying amount.
EquityDividends declared are distributions from retained earnings and create dividends payable; they are not expenses.
LiabilitiesClassify current vs non-current by settlement timing, not by account name.
Company SPL/SFPPlace gain on disposal under other income; interest under financial expenses; auditors’ and directors’ remuneration under administrative expenses.
