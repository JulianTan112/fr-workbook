Question or case data

Vista Traders reports: sales revenue $500,000; sales returns $20,000; cost of goods sold before stock adjustment $310,000; inventory shortage $6,000; selling expenses $44,000; administrative expenses $38,000; interest expense $9,000; tax expense $18,000.

Case-data check

Read the full source facts before opening the answer. Trace every figure from the case data into the calculation bridge, journal entry, ledger or statement line.

Required

  • Prepare net sales, adjusted Cost of goods sold, gross profit, operating profit, profit before tax and profit after tax.
  • Calculate gross profit margin and operating profit margin.
  • State what caused the difference between gross profit margin and operating profit margin.

Model answer — structured workings

Gross profit and operating margin interpretation

Profit bridge

Net sales

$500,000 − $20,000 = $480,000
Trace statement inputs:

Sales revenue less sales returns, allowances and discounts from the sales records.

Adjusted Cost of goods sold

$310,000 + $6,000 shortage = $316,000
Trace statement inputs:

Start with cost of goods sold from the inventory or trading schedule, then adjust for inventory shortage, surplus, write-down or obsolescence from the inventory workings.

Gross profit

$480,000 − $316,000 = $164,000
Trace statement inputs:

Sales revenue − cost of sales = gross profit.

Operating profit

$164,000 − $44,000 − $38,000 = $82,000
Trace statement inputs:

Gross profit + other income − selling or distribution expenses − administrative expenses or G&A = operating profit or EBIT area.

Profit before tax

$82,000 − $9,000 = $73,000
Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit − financial expenses or interest expense = profit before tax.

Profit after tax

$73,000 − $18,000 = $55,000
Trace statement inputs:

Profit before tax − tax expense = profit after tax. Profit after tax then feeds retained earnings.

Gross profit margin

$164,000 / $480,000 × 100 = 34.17%
Trace statement inputs:

Gross profit divided by sales revenue. Gross profit is sales revenue less cost of sales.

Operating profit margin

$82,000 / $480,000 × 100 = 17.08%
Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit divided by sales revenue.

Interpretation: Gross profit margin tests buying/selling inventory performance. Operating profit margin falls further because selling and administrative expenses consume part of gross profit.
Self-check: Tie every calculation or classification to the accounting purpose. A correct final answer without the route is not enough.

Explanation for gross-profit and operating-margin interpretation

Profit bridge

Net sales:
$500,000 − $20,000 = $480,000

Adjusted Cost of goods sold:
$310,000 + $6,000 shortage = $316,000

Gross profit:
$480,000 − $316,000 = $164,000

Operating and tax bridge

Operating profit:
$164,000 − $44,000 selling or admin − $38,000 operating expense = $82,000

Profit before tax:
$82,000 − $9,000 finance cost = $73,000

Profit after tax:
$73,000 − $18,000 tax = $55,000

Margin bridge

Gross profit margin:
$164,000 / $480,000 × 100 = 34.17%

Operating profit margin:
$82,000 / $480,000 × 100 = 17.08%

The margin drop shows that operating expenses consume part of gross profit before finance cost and tax.
