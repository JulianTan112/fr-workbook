Question or case data

Part 1 — Wei Company

On 1 January 20X3, Wei Company begins with a $30,000 credit balance in Allowance for Impairment of Accounts Receivable. On 1 February, Wei writes off $6,800 as uncollectible: Oakley Co. $900 and Brookes Co. $5,900. On 5 June, Wei unexpectedly receives the $900 from Oakley Co., previously written off.

Explanation for Wei recovery and Daley aging answer

Wei write-off bridge

Write-offs:
Oakley $900 + Brookes $5,900 = $6,800

Entry:
Dr Allowance for Impairment              6,800
    Cr Accounts Receivable                       6,800

The write-off uses the allowance and does not create a new impairment expense.

Wei recovery bridge

Reinstate Oakley:
Dr Accounts Receivable                     900
    Cr Allowance for Impairment                     900

Collect cash:
Dr Cash                                    900
    Cr Accounts Receivable                           900

Allowance after Wei transactions:
$30,000 − $6,800 + $900 = $24,100

Daley aging bridge

Required allowance:
$3,960 + $1,800 + $1,800 + $1,260 + $3,000 = $11,820

Existing allowance:
$13,600 Cr

Writeback needed:
$13,600 − $11,820 = $1,780

Entry:
Dr Allowance for Impairment              1,780
    Cr Impairment Writeback                      1,780
