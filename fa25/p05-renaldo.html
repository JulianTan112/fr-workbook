Relevant information for year-end adjustments

ItemSource factAccounting issue
iUnexpired insurance as at 31 December 20X2 amounted to $1,900.Only the unexpired portion remains as current asset; the balance becomes insurance expense.
iiSupplies of $475 were on hand as at 31 December 20X2.Only supplies on hand remain as current asset; the used portion becomes supplies expense.
iii$2,050 of salaries were unpaid as at 31 December 20X2.Accrue the salary expense and recognise the unpaid liability.
ivFees earned but not invoiced as at 31 December 20X2 totalled $4,150.Recognise accrued revenue and the receivable because service revenue has already been earned.

Required

  1. Prepare the relevant adjusting journal entries. Create additional accounts if required.
  2. Determine the balances of the accounts affected by the adjusting entries and prepare an adjusted trial balance, showing all relevant workings clearly.
  3. Prepare the Statement of Profit and Loss for the year ended 31 December 20X2, classifying expenses under the appropriate categories.
  4. Prepare the Statement of Financial Position as at 31 December 20X2.

Explanation for Renaldo Services balance day adjustments

Part (a) adjustment bridge

Insurance:
Prepaid Insurance in trial balance        $4,500
Less: unexpired insurance at year-end     (1,900)
Insurance expense to recognise             $2,600
Entry: Dr Insurance Expense $2,600; Cr Prepaid Insurance $2,600

Supplies:
Supplies in trial balance                  $1,720
Less: supplies still on hand                (475)
Supplies used during the year              $1,245
Entry: Dr Supplies Expense $1,245; Cr Supplies $1,245

Unpaid salaries:
Salary expense incurred but unpaid         $2,050
Entry: Dr Clerical Staff Salaries Expense $2,050; Cr Salaries Payable $2,050

Fees earned but not invoiced:
Revenue already earned but not billed      $4,150
Entry: Dr Fees Receivable $4,150; Cr Fees Earned $4,150

Part (b) adjusted-trial-balance bridge

Prepaid Insurance:  $4,500 − $2,600 = $1,900
Supplies:           $1,720 − $1,245 = $475
Fees Earned:        $218,400 + $4,150 = $222,550
Salaries Expense:   $110,580 + $2,050 = $112,630
New debit account:  Fees Receivable $4,150
New credit account: Salaries Payable $2,050
Adjusted trial balance check: Debit $531,200 = Credit $531,200

Part (c) SPL classification bridge

Revenue:
Fees Earned = $222,550

Selling expense:
Advertising Expense = $20,200

Administrative expenses:
$112,630 salaries + $28,250 utilities + $11,500 repairs + $4,050 miscellaneous + $2,600 insurance + $1,245 supplies = $160,275

Total expenses:
$20,200 selling + $160,275 administrative = $180,475

Net profit:
$222,550 − $180,475 = $42,075

Part (d) SFP bridge

$75,000 land + $141,500 building + $90,100 equipment = $306,600

Current assets:
$475 supplies + $21,900 Accounts Receivable + $4,150 Fees Receivable + $1,900 Prepaid Insurance + $5,700 Cash = $34,125

Total assets:
$306,600 + $34,125 = $340,725

Owner’s equity:
$298,500 capital + $42,075 net profit − $10,000 drawings = $330,575

Current liabilities:
$8,100 Accounts Payable + $2,050 Salaries Payable = $10,150

Final check:
$330,575 owner’s equity + $10,150 liabilities = $340,725

Common mistake warning

Do not dump every expense into one unclassified list when the question specifically asks for expense categories. Advertising is shown as a selling expense; clerical salaries, utilities, repairs, miscellaneous office expense, insurance and supplies are shown under administrative expenses in the suggested answer. The Statement of Financial Position must use the adjusted balances, not the original trial-balance figures.

Self-check: Part (a) journal debits and credits both total $10,045. Part (b) adjusted trial balance totals $531,200 on both sides. Part (c) net profit is $42,075. Part (d) total assets equal total owner’s equity and liabilities at $340,725.
