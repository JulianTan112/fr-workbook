Explanation for the qualitative-characteristic sorter

Due-diligence treatment check added against the relevant financial-accounting principle.

The sorter is testing whether learners can distinguish useful information from merely available information. Financial information can contain numbers and still fail if it is late, biased, unverifiable, incomplete or not comparable with prior periods.

For each card, ask: does the issue affect relevance, faithful representation, comparability, verifiability, timeliness or understandability? Then explain the decision impact on users.

Due-diligence check: do not answer only “qualitative characteristic”. Name the exact characteristic and the reason the user would be helped or misled.
