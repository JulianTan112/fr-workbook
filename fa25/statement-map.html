Formula source map

SPL & SFP item locator

Use this page whenever a formula asks for a statement figure. Instead of repeating “where to get the numbers” under every formula, this page shows the itemized layout of the Statement of Profit or Loss and Statement of Financial Position, then links formulas back to the exact line items.

How to use this page

Example: Administrative expenses are not in the Statement of Financial Position and they are not selling expenses. They sit in the Statement of Profit or Loss → Operating expenses → Administrative expenses.

When a formula says “Operating profit”, “Current assets”, “Ordinary shareholders’ equity” or “Interest expense”, click the formula’s statement-map link and locate the relevant line below.

Formula source check: not every formula comes from the SPL/SFP

Use the SPL/SFP map only for statement line items. Some accounting checks and formulas come from journals, ledgers, share records, tax computations or market data.

Ledger / trial balanceTotal debits = total credits

Comes from posted ledger balances before statements are prepared.

Adjustment worksheetPrepaids, accruals and depreciation

Use the case data first, then post the SPL/SFP effect.

Share recordsWeighted-average ordinary shares

Used for EPS and book value per share; not an SPL line item.

Market dataMarket price per share

Used for dividend yield and P/E ratio; not found inside financial statements.

Tax computationTaxable income

May differ from accounting profit before tax unless the question says to use accounting profit.

Purchase / sales recordsCredit purchases and credit sales

Needed for receivable/payable period ratios when not all sales or purchases are on credit.

Statement of Profit or Loss itemized layout

The SPL reports performance for a period. Formula inputs such as sales revenue, cost of sales, gross profit, operating profit, interest expense, profit before tax, tax expense and profit after tax come from here.

Sales revenue / RevenueTop line of SPLUsed by: gross profit margin, operating profit margin, receivables turnover

Less: Cost of sales (COGS)

Opening inventoryInventory balance brought forward from prior period / inventory records
Add: Purchases / net purchasesPurchase records; used for payables settlement period when credit purchases are given
Add: Freight-in / carriage-inBuyer’s cost to bring inventory to location and condition for sale
Less: Purchase returns / allowances / discountsReduces inventory purchase cost
Goods available for saleOpening inventory + net purchases + freight-in
Less: Closing inventoryInventory counted/valued at reporting date; also appears in SFP current assets
Add: Inventory shortage / write-off / obsolescenceIncreases expense or reduces inventory where inventory is missing, obsolete or below NRV
Cost of sales (COGS)Used by gross profit, gross profit margin, inventory turnover
Gross profitSales revenue − Cost of salesBefore selling, administrative and finance expenses

Add: Other income

Commission revenue / rental income / interest incomeIncome outside main trading sales, depending on question
Gain on disposal of non-current assetProceeds excluding GST − carrying amount; credit in SPL

Less: Operating expenses

Selling / distribution / marketing expenses

Salesmen commissionSelling expense
Delivery expense / freight-out / transportation-outSeller’s distribution cost, not inventory cost
Advertising / marketing expenseSelling/marketing expense
Showroom rent / sales salariesSelling expense when tied to sales/showroom function
Depreciation expense — delivery vehicles / store equipmentSelling/distribution depreciation where asset supports selling or delivery

Administrative expenses / G&A

Administrative salaries expenseGeneral and administrative expense
Directors’ remunerationAdministrative expense unless question says otherwise
Auditors’ remunerationAdministrative expense
Office rent / office utility / office supplies expenseAdministrative expense
Depreciation expense — office equipmentAdministrative depreciation where asset supports office/admin function
Impairment of accounts receivable / bad debts expenseAdministrative expense in the course layout unless question specifies selling expense
Operating profit / EBIT areaGross profit + Other income − Selling expenses − Administrative expensesBefore finance costs and tax in the course-style layout

Less: Financial expenses

Interest expense on loan / bonds / debenturesUsed in interest cover and PBT bridge
Profit before taxOperating profit − Financial expenses

Less: Tax expense

Current-year tax expenseSPL expense; corresponding credit may be Tax payable in SFP
Adjustment for prior-year taxAdjusts current SPL tax expense depending on under/over provision
Profit after tax / Net profitFeeds retained earnings and equity; used in EPS, ROE/ROSF and dividend payout

Statement of Financial Position itemized layout

The SFP reports position at a point in time. Formula inputs such as current assets, current liabilities, inventory, trade receivables, cash, share capital, retained earnings, borrowings and capital employed come from here.

ASSETS

Non-current assets

Property, plant and equipment: delivery vehicles / office equipment / machinery / buildingsShown at cost less accumulated depreciation
Less: Accumulated depreciationContra-asset deducted from PPE cost to show carrying amount
Intangible assets / investmentsNon-current asset section where relevant

Current assets

InventoryClosing inventory after shortage, surplus, NRV/obsolescence adjustments
Less: Allowance for inventory obsolescenceContra account if allowance presentation is used
Accounts receivable / trade receivablesGross receivables before allowance
Less: Allowance for impairment of A/RContra account deducted to present net receivables
Quick assetsCash + short-term investments + net receivables; excludes inventory and prepaids for acid-test logic
Prepaid expensesAsset for benefits paid in advance but not yet consumed
Cash at bank / petty cashCurrent asset; use adjusted cash balance after bank reconciliation
Total assetsNon-current assets + Current assets

EQUITY & LIABILITIES

Equity / share capital and reserves

Share capitalIssued ordinary/preference share capital
Ordinary sharesOrdinary share capital; shares outstanding may be in notes/share records
Preference sharesEquity if not redeemable as liability; preference dividends affect EPS numerator
Retained earnings / reservesOpening retained earnings + profit after tax − dividends
Dividends declaredDeducted from retained earnings; unpaid dividends appear as dividends payable under current liabilities
Ordinary shareholders’ equityTotal equity attributable to ordinary shareholders, after excluding preference claims where relevant

Non-current liabilities

Bank loan / long-term borrowingNon-current portion of borrowing
Bonds / debenturesLong-term financing liability; related interest expense is in SPL
Redeemable preference sharesMay be liability-style where redemption obligation exists
Capital employedEquity + Non-current liabilitiesUsed in ROCE and sales revenue to capital employed

Current liabilities

Accounts payable / trade payablesAmounts owed to suppliers; used in payables settlement period
Dividends payableDeclared but unpaid dividends
Tax payableCurrent liability owed to tax authority; not the SPL tax expense line
GST payable / output GST net of input GSTAmount owed to IRAS/tax authority, depending on GST control balance
Unearned income / revenue received in advanceLiability until goods/services are provided
Accrued expenses / accrued salaries / accrued interestExpenses incurred but unpaid
Bank overdraft / current portion of loanCurrent financing obligation where repayable within one year
Total equity and liabilitiesEquity + Non-current liabilities + Current liabilities

Statement of Cash Flows mini-locator

Most v25 journal-entry questions focus on SPL/SFP. For interpretation formulas, free cash flow uses the Statement of Cash Flows:

Cash flow from operating activitiesCash generated from core operations
Capital expenditure / purchase of PPEUsually appears in investing cash flows; used when calculating free cash flow
Cash flow from financing activitiesShare issues, borrowings, loan repayments and dividends paid where relevant

Formula-to-statement locator

Each formula below points to the line items above. The formula is highlighted; the statement links show where the input comes from.

L04/L06

Gross profit

FormulaSales revenue − Cost of sales (COGS)
Trace statement inputs:

Sales revenue − cost of sales (COGS) = gross profit.

How to read it:

Use before deducting selling, administrative, finance and tax expenses.

L04/L06

Gross profit margin

FormulaGross profit ÷ Sales revenue × 100
Trace statement inputs:

Gross profit ÷ sales revenue. Gross profit is sales revenue less cost of sales.

How to read it:

Shows the percentage of sales left after inventory cost.

L10

Operating profit

FormulaGross profit + Other income − Selling expenses − Administrative expenses
Trace statement inputs:

Gross profit + other income − selling/distribution expenses − administrative expenses / G&A = operating profit / EBIT area.

How to read it:

Shows operating result before finance costs and tax.

L10

Operating profit margin

FormulaOperating profit ÷ Sales revenue × 100
Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit ÷ sales revenue.

How to read it:

Shows operating return per sales dollar.

L09/L10

ROCE — Return on Capital Employed

FormulaOperating profit ÷ Capital employed × 100
Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit ÷ capital employed. Capital employed is normally equity plus non-current liabilities.

How to read it:

Connects operating profit to long-term funds employed.

L10

Current ratio

FormulaCurrent assets ÷ Current liabilities
Trace statement inputs:

Current assets ÷ current liabilities.

How to read it:

Measures short-term liquidity.

L10

Acid-test / Quick ratio

FormulaQuick assets ÷ Current liabilities
Trace statement inputs:

Quick assets ÷ current liabilities. Quick assets exclude inventory.

How to read it:

Stricter liquidity test excluding inventory.

L06

Inventory turnover

FormulaCost of sales ÷ Average inventory
Trace statement inputs:

Use cost of sales / COGS and average inventory. Average inventory normally uses opening and closing inventory.

How to read it:

Measures how frequently inventory is sold.

L06

Inventory turnover period

FormulaAverage inventory ÷ Cost of sales × 365
Trace statement inputs:

Use cost of sales / COGS and average inventory. Average inventory normally uses opening and closing inventory.

How to read it:

Converts inventory turnover into average days held.

L07

Receivables collection period

FormulaAverage trade receivables ÷ Credit sales × 365
Trace source:

Use average trade receivables from SFP trade receivables and credit sales from sales records / SPL sales if all sales are credit.

How to read it:

Measures average days customers take to pay.

L07/L10

Payables settlement period

FormulaAverage trade payables ÷ Credit purchases × 365
Trace source:

Use average trade payables from the SFP and credit purchases from purchase records. If credit purchases are not given, use the amount specified by the question.

How to read it:

Measures average days taken to pay suppliers.

L07/L10

Operating cash cycle

FormulaInventory days + Receivable days − Payable days
Trace source:

This combines three separate working-capital timing measures: inventory days + receivable days − payable days. The components are built from SPL/SFP inputs and credit records.

How to read it:

Shows how long cash is tied up in working capital.

L10

Gearing ratio

FormulaDebt ÷ (Equity + Debt) × 100
Trace statement inputs:

Debt / borrowings ÷ (share capital + reserves/retained earnings + debt).

How to read it:

Shows long-term finance from lenders versus owners.

L10

Interest cover

FormulaOperating profit ÷ Interest expense
Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit ÷ interest expense / finance cost.

How to read it:

Shows ability to cover finance costs.

L09

EPS — Earnings per Share

Formula(Profit after tax − Preference dividends) ÷ Weighted-average ordinary shares
Trace source:

Profit after tax minus preference dividends from equity/dividend records, divided by weighted-average ordinary shares from the share records. Market price is not used in EPS.

How to read it:

Shows profit attributable to each ordinary share.

L09

Dividend payout ratio

FormulaOrdinary dividends ÷ Earnings available to ordinary shareholders × 100
Trace source:

Ordinary dividends from dividend/equity records ÷ earnings available to ordinary shareholders from SPL profit after tax adjusted for preference dividends.

How to read it:

Shows how much earnings are distributed as dividends.

L09

Dividend yield

FormulaDividend per share ÷ Market price per share × 100
Trace source:

Dividend per share comes from dividend records; market price per share is outside the financial statements. Do not look for market price inside the SPL or SFP.

How to read it:

Investor yield based on market price; market price is outside the statements.

L09

P/E ratio — Price/Earnings ratio

FormulaMarket price per share ÷ EPS
Trace source:

Market price per share is outside the financial statements. EPS is calculated from profit after tax, preference dividends and ordinary shares.

How to read it:

Shows how many times annual earnings investors are paying.

L09

Book value per share

FormulaOrdinary shareholders’ equity ÷ Ordinary shares outstanding
Trace statement inputs:

Ordinary shareholders’ equity ÷ ordinary shares outstanding.

How to read it:

Accounting net asset backing per ordinary share.

Cash flows

Free cash flow

FormulaCash flow from operating activities − Capital expenditure
Trace source:

Use operating cash flow from the Statement of Cash Flows and capital expenditure from investing activities / PPE additions. This is a cash-flow interpretation measure, not an SPL/SFP-only figure.

How to read it:

Interpretive cash measure, not a journal entry.
