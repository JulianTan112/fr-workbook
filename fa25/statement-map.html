Formula source check: not every formula comes from the SPL or SFP

Use the SPL or SFP map only for statement line items. Some accounting checks and formulas come from journals, ledgers, share records, tax computations or market data.

Ledger or trial balanceTotal debits = total credits

Comes from posted ledger balances before statements are prepared.

Adjustment worksheetPrepaids, accruals and depreciation

Use the case data first, then post the SPL or SFP effect.

Share recordsWeighted-average ordinary shares

Used for EPS and book value per share; not an SPL line item.

Market dataMarket price per share

Used for dividend yield and Price-earnings ratio; not found inside financial statements.

Tax computationTaxable income

May differ from accounting profit before tax unless the question says to use accounting profit.

Purchase or sales recordsCredit purchases and credit sales

Needed for receivable or payable period ratios when not all sales or purchases are on credit.

Terminology guardrail: Cost of sales vs cost of goods sold

Use “Cost of sales” as the SPL line item. Use “Cost of goods sold” only when the question is asking for the inventory or trading-business working that transfers the cost of inventory sold out of Inventory and into expense.

Cost of salesSPL presentation line. It is the expense deducted from revenue before gross profit in the statement layout.
Cost of goods soldInventory or trading calculation. It is derived from opening inventory, purchases, freight-in, returns, discounts, closing inventory and inventory adjustments.
Workbook ruleStatement locator = Cost of sales. Inventory ledger or trading schedule = Cost of goods sold. Do not attach an inventory abbreviation to the SPL line.

The SPL reports performance for a period. Formula inputs such as sales revenue, cost of sales, gross profit, operating profit, interest expense, profit before tax, tax expense and profit after tax come from here.

Add: Freight-in or carriage-inBuyer’s cost to bring inventory to location and condition for sale

Goods available for saleOpening inventory + net purchases + freight-in
Less: Closing inventoryInventory counted or valued at reporting date; also appears in SFP current assets

Gross profitSales revenue − Cost of salesBefore selling, administrative and finance expenses

Statement of Financial Position itemized layout

The SFP reports position at a point in time. Formula inputs such as current assets, current liabilities, inventory, trade receivables, cash, share capital, retained earnings, borrowings and capital employed come from here.

ASSETS

Non-current assets

Property, plant and equipment: delivery vehicles or office equipment or machinery or buildingsShown at cost less accumulated depreciation
Less: Accumulated depreciationContra-asset deducted from PPE cost to show carrying amount
Intangible assets or investmentsNon-current asset section where relevant

Current assets

InventoryClosing inventory after shortage, surplus, NRV or obsolescence adjustments
Less: Allowance for inventory obsolescenceContra account if allowance presentation is used
Accounts receivable or trade receivablesGross receivables before allowance
Less: Allowance for impairment of Accounts ReceivableContra account deducted to present net receivables
Quick assetsCash + short-term investments + net receivables; excludes inventory and prepaids for acid-test logic
Prepaid expensesAsset for benefits paid in advance but not yet consumed
Cash at bank or petty cashCurrent asset; use adjusted cash balance after bank reconciliation
Total assetsNon-current assets + Current assets

EQUITY & LIABILITIES

Equity or share capital and reserves

Share capitalIssued ordinary or preference share capital
Ordinary sharesOrdinary share capital; shares outstanding may be in notes or share records
Preference sharesEquity if not redeemable as liability; preference dividends affect EPS numerator
Retained earnings or reservesOpening retained earnings + profit after tax − dividends
Dividends declaredDeducted from retained earnings; unpaid dividends appear as dividends payable under current liabilities
Ordinary shareholders’ equityTotal equity attributable to ordinary shareholders, after excluding preference claims where relevant

Non-current liabilities

Bank loan or long-term borrowingNon-current portion of borrowing
Bonds or debenturesLong-term financing liability; related interest expense is in SPL
Redeemable preference sharesMay be liability-style where redemption obligation exists
Capital employedEquity + Non-current liabilitiesUsed in ROCE and sales revenue to capital employed

Current liabilities

Accounts payable or trade payablesAmounts owed to suppliers; used in payables settlement period
Dividends payableDeclared but unpaid dividends
Tax payableCurrent liability owed to tax authority; not the SPL tax expense line
GST payable or output GST net of input GSTAmount owed to IRAS or tax authority, depending on GST control balance
Unearned income or revenue received in advanceLiability until goods or services are provided
Accrued expenses or accrued salaries or accrued interestExpenses incurred but unpaid
Bank overdraft or current portion of loanCurrent financing obligation where repayable within one year
Total equity and liabilitiesEquity + Non-current liabilities + Current liabilities

Statement of Cash Flows mini-locator

The Statement of Cash Flows is not an SPL or SFP formula source only. It classifies cash movements into operating, investing and financing activities.

Operating activitiesCash flows from core trading or income-generating operations. Indirect method adjusts net profit for non-cash items and working-capital movements.
Investing activitiesCash paid for PPE or long-term investments, and cash proceeds from disposal of PPE or long-term investments.
Financing activitiesCash from owners and lenders, and cash payments to owners and lenders: share issues, borrowings, loan repayments and dividends paid where relevant.
Non-cash investing and financing activitiesExamples: acquiring assets by issuing shares or converting debt into equity. Disclose separately if required; do not include in cash totals.
PPE disposal trap: Gain or loss goes to SPL, but the cash proceeds from selling PPE go to investing activities. See PPE disposal cash-flow bridge.

Formula-to-statement locator

Each formula below points to the line items above. The formula is highlighted; the statement links show where the input comes from.

L04 or L06

Gross profit

FormulaSales revenue − Cost of sales
Trace statement inputs:

Sales revenue − cost of sales = gross profit.

How to read it:

Use before deducting selling, administrative, finance and tax expenses.

L04 or L06

Gross profit margin

FormulaGross profit / Sales revenue × 100
Trace statement inputs:

Gross profit divided by sales revenue. Gross profit is sales revenue less cost of sales.

How to read it:

Shows the percentage of sales left after inventory cost.

L10

Operating profit

FormulaGross profit + Other income − Selling expenses − Administrative expenses
Trace statement inputs:

Gross profit + other income − selling or distribution expenses − administrative expenses or G&A = operating profit or EBIT area.

How to read it:

Shows operating result before finance costs and tax.

L10

Operating profit margin

FormulaOperating profit / Sales revenue × 100
Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit divided by sales revenue.

How to read it:

Shows operating return per sales dollar.

L09 or L10

ROCE — Return on Capital Employed

FormulaOperating profit / Capital employed × 100
Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit divided by capital employed. Capital employed is normally equity plus non-current liabilities.

How to read it:

Connects operating profit to long-term funds employed.

L10

Current ratio

FormulaCurrent assets / Current liabilities
Trace statement inputs:

Current assets divided by current liabilities.

How to read it:

Measures short-term liquidity.

L10

Acid-test or quick ratio

FormulaQuick assets / Current liabilities
Trace statement inputs:

Quick assets divided by current liabilities. Quick assets exclude inventory.

How to read it:

Stricter liquidity test excluding inventory.

L06

Inventory turnover

FormulaCost of goods sold / Average inventory
Trace inputs:

Use cost of goods sold from the inventory or trading schedule and average inventory. Do not rename the SPL line “Cost of sales” as “Cost of goods sold” in this map.

How to read it:

Measures how frequently inventory is sold.

L06

Inventory turnover period

FormulaAverage inventory / Cost of goods sold × 365
Trace inputs:

Use average inventory and cost of goods sold from the inventory or trading schedule. This is an inventory efficiency ratio, not a label-change instruction for the SPL.

How to read it:

Converts inventory turnover into average days held.

L07

Receivables collection period

FormulaAverage trade receivables / Credit sales × 365
Trace source:

Use average trade receivables from SFP trade receivables and credit sales from sales records or SPL sales if all sales are credit.

How to read it:

Measures average days customers take to pay.

L07 or L10

Payables settlement period

FormulaAverage trade payables / Credit purchases × 365
Trace source:

Use average trade payables from the SFP and credit purchases from purchase records. If credit purchases are not given, use the amount specified by the question.

How to read it:

Measures average days taken to pay suppliers.

L07 or L10

Operating cash cycle

FormulaInventory days + Receivable days − Payable days
Trace source:

This combines three separate working-capital timing measures: inventory days + receivable days − payable days. The components are built from SPL or SFP inputs and credit records.

How to read it:

Shows how long cash is tied up in working capital.

L10

Gearing ratio

FormulaDebt / (Equity + Debt) × 100
Trace statement inputs:

Debt or borrowings divided by (share capital + reserves or retained earnings + debt).

How to read it:

Shows long-term finance from lenders versus owners.

L10

Interest cover

FormulaOperating profit / Interest expense
Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit divided by interest expense or finance cost.

How to read it:

Shows ability to cover finance costs.

L09

EPS — Earnings per Share

Formula(Profit after tax − Preference dividends) / Weighted-average ordinary shares
Trace source:

Profit after tax minus preference dividends from equity or dividend records, divided by weighted-average ordinary shares from the share records. Market price is not used in EPS.

How to read it:

Shows profit attributable to each ordinary share.

L09

Dividend payout ratio

FormulaOrdinary dividends / Earnings available to ordinary shareholders × 100
Trace source:

Ordinary dividends from dividend or equity records or earnings available to ordinary shareholders from SPL profit after tax adjusted for preference dividends.

How to read it:

Shows how much earnings are distributed as dividends.

L09

Dividend yield

FormulaDividend per share / Market price per share × 100
Trace source:

Dividend per share comes from dividend records; market price per share is outside the financial statements. Do not look for market price inside the SPL or SFP.

How to read it:

Investor yield based on market price; market price is outside the statements.

L09

Price-earnings ratio

FormulaMarket price per share / EPS
Trace source:

Market price per share is outside the financial statements. EPS is calculated from profit after tax, preference dividends and ordinary shares.

How to read it:

Shows how many times annual earnings investors are paying.

L09

Book value per share

FormulaOrdinary shareholders’ equity / Ordinary shares outstanding
Trace statement inputs:

Ordinary shareholders’ equity divided by ordinary shares outstanding.

How to read it:

Accounting net asset backing per ordinary share.

Cash flows

Free cash flow

FormulaCash flow from operating activities − Capital expenditure
Trace source:

Use cash flow from operating activities and capital expenditure from investing activities. This is a cash-flow interpretation measure, not an SPL or SFP only figure.

How to read it:

Interpretive cash measure, not a journal entry.
