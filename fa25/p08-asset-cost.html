Explanation for asset-cost boundary answer

Where the $154,300 comes from

The $154,300 is taken from the case facts above. It is not a hidden amount from the journal entry. The journal only records the total after deciding which costs are directly attributable to bringing the machine to the required location and condition for use.

Capitalised initial machine cost:
Purchase price                            $150,000
Transport to site                              800
Installation                                  2,000
Trial testing before use                      1,500
Total PPE cost                              $154,300

Why annual insurance is excluded here

The excluded item is annual insurance after the machine is ready for use. That is a post-readiness period cost. It does not make the machine ready for use, so it is not part of the initial PPE cost.

Boundary warning: insurance during transportation or installation can be different. If the facts say insurance is incurred while the machine is being transported or prepared for use, it may be directly attributable. This practice question says annual insurance after readiness, so it is expensed instead of capitalised.

Journal bridge

Dr Property, Plant and Equipment — Machine      154,300
    Cr Cash or Accounts Payable                           154,300

The debit records the machine as a non-current asset in the SFP. The credit records how the asset was financed or paid. Annual insurance, staff training and routine post-use repair are not included in this debit; they are expenses unless a later cost improves capacity, productivity, useful life or future economic benefits.

Cash-flow angle

If the machine is paid in cash, the capitalised machine purchase is an investing activity. Annual insurance, training and routine repairs paid in cash are operating cash flows because they are period operating costs, not PPE acquisition cash flows.
