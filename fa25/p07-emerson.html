Cash & Receivables — Additional Questions, Question 1

On 30 September 20X1, Emerson Co. has $540,250 of accounts receivable. Emerson uses the allowance method of accounting for uncollectible debts and has an existing credit balance in the allowance for impairment of accounts receivable of $23,750.

The company uses the perpetual inventory system and had the following transactions in October 20X1:

ItemOriginal source fact
iSold $325,000 of merchandise that cost $178,500 to customers on credit.
iiReceived $425,100 cash in payment of accounts receivable.
iiiWrote off $16,700 of uncollectible accounts receivable.
ivIn adjusting the accounts on October 31, its fiscal year-end, the company estimated that 4% of accounts receivable will be uncollectible.
Source-trace note

The source question requires October journal entries and the SFP presentation of Accounts Receivable and Allowance for Impairment of Accounts Receivable. The model answer may add ledger bridges to show how the final figures are derived, but those are explanation support, not extra required tasks.

Required

  1. Prepare journal entries to record the transactions in October 20X1. Narrations are not required.
  2. Show how Accounts Receivable and the Allowance for Impairment of Accounts Receivable would appear on Emerson Co.’s Statement of Financial Position as at 31 October 20X1.

Explanation for Emerson allowance method

Accounts Receivable bridge: begin with $540,250. Add credit sales of $325,000. Deduct cash collections of $425,100. Deduct the write-off of $16,700.

$540,250 + $325,000 − $425,100 − $16,700 = $423,450

Allowance bridge: after the write-off, the allowance has a remaining credit balance of $7,050.

$23,750 − $16,700 = $7,050 credit

The required ending allowance is 4% of the final Accounts Receivable balance.

$423,450 × 4% = $16,938 required allowance

Therefore the year-end impairment adjustment is the balancing amount needed to bring the allowance from $7,050 credit to $16,938 credit.

$16,938 − $7,050 = $9,888 impairment loss

Journal logic: the write-off does not create a fresh impairment expense because the allowance method already estimated the expected loss earlier. The write-off uses the allowance against Accounts Receivable. The fresh expense is recorded only for the year-end estimate adjustment.

SFP presentation: Accounts Receivable $423,450 less Allowance for Impairment of Accounts Receivable $16,938 gives net receivables of $406,512.
