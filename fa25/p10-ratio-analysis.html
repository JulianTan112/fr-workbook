Question / case data

Northline Ltd reports current assets $240,000 including inventory $90,000; current liabilities $120,000; non-current liabilities $300,000; share capital $400,000; reserves $200,000; operating profit $96,000; interest payable $24,000.

Case-data check

Case-data audit: review all listed dates, amounts, assumptions and required tasks before revealing the model answer. The answer depends on classification as well as calculation.

Required

  • Calculate current ratio and acid-test ratio.
  • Calculate gearing ratio.
  • Calculate interest cover.
  • State the accounting meaning of each result.

Model answer — structured workings

Liquidity, gearing and interest-cover drill

Liquidity and solvency calculations

Current ratio

$240,000 ÷ $120,000 = 2.0 : 1

Current assets cover current liabilities twice over.

Trace statement inputs:

Current assets ÷ current liabilities.

Acid-test ratio

($240,000 − $90,000) ÷ $120,000 = 1.25 : 1

Inventory is removed for the stricter liquidity test.

Trace statement inputs:

Quick assets ÷ current liabilities. Quick assets exclude inventory.

Gearing ratio

$300,000 ÷ ($400,000 + $200,000 + $300,000) × 100 = 33.33%

Shows non-current lender financing as part of long-term capital structure.

Trace statement inputs:

Debt / borrowings ÷ (share capital + reserves/retained earnings + debt).

Interest cover

$96,000 ÷ $24,000 = 4 times

Operating profit covers interest four times.

Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit ÷ interest expense / finance cost.

Interpretation: Liquidity ratios use current assets/liabilities. Gearing and interest cover interpret long-term debt risk and ability to meet finance costs.
Self-check: Tie every calculation or classification to the accounting purpose. A correct final answer without the route is not enough.

Explanation for liquidity, gearing and interest-cover answer

Due-diligence treatment check added against the relevant financial-accounting principle.

Liquidity ratios use SFP current assets and current liabilities. Gearing and interest cover focus on financing risk: how much long-term funding comes from debt and whether operating profit can cover finance cost.

Ratio answers are incomplete without interpretation. State whether the ratio suggests stronger/weaker liquidity, higher/lower debt risk, or better/weaker ability to service interest.

Due-diligence check: current ratio and acid-test ratio use SFP figures; interest cover uses operating profit and finance cost from the SPL.
