Liquidity, gearing and interest-cover drill

Liquidity and solvency calculations

Current ratio

$240,000 / $120,000 = 2.0 : 1

Current assets cover current liabilities twice over.

Trace statement inputs:

Current assets divided by current liabilities.

Acid-test ratio

($240,000 − $90,000) / $120,000 = 1.25 : 1

Inventory is removed for the stricter liquidity test.

Trace statement inputs:

Quick assets divided by current liabilities. Quick assets exclude inventory.

Gearing ratio

$300,000 / ($400,000 + $200,000 + $300,000) × 100 = 33.33%

Shows non-current lender financing as part of long-term capital structure.

Trace statement inputs:

Debt or borrowings divided by (share capital + reserves or retained earnings + debt).

Interest cover

$96,000 / $24,000 = 4 times

Operating profit covers interest four times.

Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit divided by interest expense or finance cost.

Interpretation: Liquidity ratios use current assets or liabilities. Gearing and interest cover interpret long-term debt risk and ability to meet finance costs.
Self-check: Tie every calculation or classification to the accounting purpose. A correct final answer without the route is not enough.

Explanation for liquidity, gearing and interest-cover answer

Liquidity bridge

Current ratio:
Current assets $240,000 / current liabilities $120,000 = 2.0 : 1

Acid-test ratio:
($240,000 − inventory $90,000) / $120,000 = 1.25 : 1

Gearing bridge

Gearing:
Non-current debt $300,000 / (share capital $400,000 + reserves $200,000 + non-current debt $300,000) × 100
= $300,000 / $900,000 × 100
= 33.33%

Interest-cover bridge

Interest cover:
Operating profit $96,000 / interest expense $24,000 = 4 times

Liquidity ratios use current SFP items. Gearing uses long-term capital structure. Interest cover links SPL operating profit to finance cost capacity.
