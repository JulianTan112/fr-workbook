Question or case data

Cases: a report is issued too late; management uses biased estimates; the business changes methods without explanation; an amount can be checked against invoices; a note omits a material uncertainty.

Case-data check

Case-data audit: review all listed dates, amounts, assumptions and required tasks before revealing the model answer. The answer depends on classification as well as calculation.

Required

  • Classify the characteristic or constraint affected.
  • State why the information becomes less useful.

Qualitative characteristics and constraints mini-cases

Answer route

Report issued too late

CharacteristicTimeliness

Late information may lose decision usefulness.

Biased estimate

CharacteristicFaithful representation or neutrality

Biased measurement does not faithfully represent the economic item.

Method changed without explanation

CharacteristicComparability or consistency

Users cannot compare periods properly.

Amount traceable to invoices

CharacteristicVerifiability

Independent checks can support the amount.

Material uncertainty omitted

CharacteristicCompleteness or relevance

Missing explanations can change users’ decisions.

Explanation for qualitative characteristics and constraints

Case-fact bridge

Report issued too late             → Timeliness
Biased management estimate          → Faithful representation or neutrality
Method changed without explanation  → Comparability or consistency
Amount traceable to invoices        → Verifiability
Material uncertainty omitted        → Completeness or relevance

This is a classification answer, not a journal answer. The mark comes from tying each fact to the characteristic affected. For example, late information can be less useful even if mathematically accurate, while a biased estimate fails neutrality.

Self-check: do not write a keyword alone. Use the pattern: case fact → characteristic → why users are affected.
