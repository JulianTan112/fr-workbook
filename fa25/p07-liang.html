Explanation for Liang two-year allowance method

Year 1 Accounts Receivable and allowance bridge

Ending Accounts Receivable:
Sales $1,345,434 − write-off $18,300 − collections $669,200 = $657,934

Required allowance:
$657,934 × 1.5% = $9,869 rounded

Allowance adjustment:
Write-off creates $18,300 debit balance.
To reach $9,869 credit, credit adjustment = $18,300 + $9,869 = $28,169

Year 2 bridge

Ending Accounts Receivable:
$657,934 + $1,525,634 − $27,800 − $1,204,600 = $951,168

Required allowance:
$951,168 × 1.5% = $14,268

Allowance before adjustment:
$9,869 Cr − $27,800 write-off = $17,931 Dr

Required credit adjustment:
$17,931 + $14,268 = $32,199

The allowance adjustment is based on the T-account movement needed to reach the required ending allowance, not simply the percentage amount alone.
