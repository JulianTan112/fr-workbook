2. High-risk exam traps by lesson

AreaTrap check
Regulatory or EthicsName the threat exactly: self-interest, self-review, advocacy, familiarity or intimidation. Then identify why judgement or objectivity is at risk.
Accounting EquationCollection from a credit customer is not new revenue. It is Dr Cash or Cr Accounts Receivable only.
Recording TransactionsUse AED LRC first. Then journalise, post to ledger, prepare trial balance, then statements.
Trading BusinessUnder perpetual inventory, sales of goods usually need two entries: revenue side and cost side.
FCA or CPTGoods shipped before year-end are not automatically buyer inventory. Check ownership transfer: FCA origin or handed to carrier vs CPT destination or arrival.
Adjusting EntriesStandard balance-day adjustments generally do not use Cash at Bank.
InventoryPhysical possession is not ownership. Consignment goods remain inventory of consignor until third-party sale.
Bank ReconciliationDeposits-in-transit and unpresented cheques are bank-side timing items; bank charges, EFT receipts and dishonoured cheques are book-side journal items.
ReceivablesAllowance write-off does not create a new impairment expense; Dr Allowance or Cr Accounts Receivable.
PPEAccumulated depreciation is a contra-asset, not a liability.
GST disposalGST is calculated once on selling price excluding GST. Gain is sale proceeds excluding GST less carrying amount.
EquityDividends declared are distributions from retained earnings and create dividends payable; they are not expenses.
LiabilitiesClassify current vs non-current by settlement timing, not by account name.
Company SPL or SFPPlace gain on disposal under other income; interest under financial expenses; auditors’ and directors’ remuneration under administrative expenses.

6. Term traps that change the answer format

Term trapWhat the examiner is testingSafe answer move
EBIT vs Profit before taxWhether interest or finance expense has already been deducted.EBIT excludes interest and tax; PBT excludes tax only.
Operating profit vs Profit after taxWhether the learner deducts tax too early.Operating profit comes before finance expenses and tax in the course company SPL logic.
Taxable income vs accounting profitWhether tax is calculated on the correct base.Use taxable income when provided. Do not assume it equals accounting PBT unless question says so.
Impairment expense vs allowanceSPL vs SFP separation.Expense or loss goes SPL; allowance or contra account deducts the asset in SFP.
Contra account vs liabilityClassification and presentation.Allowance and accumulated depreciation are deductions from assets, not external liabilities.

Source-reinforced exam route map

Use this route when questions mix Financial Accounting treatment with interpretation formulas.

Step 1 — identify answer type

Journalise, prepare statement, reconcile, calculate ratio, or explain framework. Do not use a paragraph when the question asks for a table/schedule.

Step 2 — choose the accounting object

Transaction account, statement line, contra account, liability or equity item, or interpretation ratio.

Step 3 — format before numbers

Set up the journal table, reconciliation layout, stock card, statement bridge or formula card before filling figures.

Step 4 — interpret only after calculation

For ratios, state what the formula measures: profitability, liquidity, efficiency, gearing, investor return or cash flow.

High-frequency formula groups

Gross profit

Sales revenue − Cost of sales

Used before operating expenses. Inventory errors and Cost of goods sold adjustments flow here.

Trace statement inputs:

Sales revenue − cost of sales = gross profit.

Gross profit margin

Gross profit / Sales revenue × 100

Shows how much sales revenue remains after inventory cost.

Trace statement inputs:

Gross profit divided by sales revenue. Gross profit is sales revenue less cost of sales.

Operating profit margin

Operating profit / Sales revenue × 100

Shows operating return after operating expenses, before finance costs and tax in common course layouts.

Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit divided by sales revenue.

ROCE

Operating profit / Capital employed × 100

Connects operating profit to long-term funds employed.

Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit divided by capital employed. Capital employed is normally equity plus non-current liabilities.

Current ratio

Current assets / Current liabilities

Liquidity measure; higher is not automatically better if cash or inventory is idle.

Trace statement inputs:

Current assets divided by current liabilities.

Acid-test or quick ratio

(Current assets − Inventories) or Current liabilities

Stricter liquidity test because inventory may not convert to cash quickly.

Trace statement inputs:

Quick assets divided by current liabilities. Quick assets exclude inventory.

Receivables collection period

Average trade receivables / Credit sales × 365

Measures average collection time from customers.

Trace source:

Use average trade receivables from SFP trade receivables and credit sales from sales records or SPL sales if all sales are credit.

Inventory turnover period

Average inventories / Cost of sales × 365

Measures average days inventory is held.

Trace statement inputs:

Use cost of goods sold from the inventory or trading schedule and average inventory. Average inventory normally uses opening and closing inventory.

Payables settlement period

Average trade payables / Credit purchases × 365

Measures average time taken to pay suppliers.

Trace source:

Use average trade payables from the SFP and credit purchases from purchase records. If credit purchases are not given, use the amount specified by the question.

Operating cash cycle

Inventory days + Receivable days − Payable days

Shows how long cash is tied up in working capital.

Trace source:

This combines three separate working-capital timing measures: inventory days + receivable days − payable days. The components are built from SPL or SFP inputs and credit records.

Gearing ratio

Non-current liabilities / (Share capital + Reserves + Non-current liabilities) × 100

Measures long-term lender contribution to capital structure.

Trace statement inputs:

Debt or borrowings divided by (share capital + reserves or retained earnings + debt).

Interest cover

Operating profit / Interest payable

Shows how many times operating profit covers interest.

Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit divided by interest expense or finance cost.

EPS

(Profit after tax − Preference dividends) or Weighted average ordinary shares

Profit attributable to each ordinary share.

Trace source:

Profit after tax minus preference dividends from equity or dividend records, divided by weighted-average ordinary shares from the share records. Market price is not used in EPS.

Dividend payout ratio

Ordinary dividends / Earnings available for ordinary shareholders × 100

Shows how much profit is distributed as dividends.

Trace source:

Ordinary dividends from dividend or equity records or earnings available to ordinary shareholders from SPL profit after tax adjusted for preference dividends.

Dividend yield

Dividend per share / Market price per share × 100

Investor cash return based on current market price.

Trace source:

Dividend per share comes from dividend records; market price per share is outside the financial statements. Do not look for market price inside the SPL or SFP.

Price-earnings ratio

Market price per share / Earnings per share

Shows how many times earnings investors are paying for the share.

Trace source:

Market price per share is outside the financial statements. EPS is calculated from profit after tax, preference dividends and ordinary shares.

Book value per share

Ordinary shareholders’ equity / Ordinary shares outstanding

Accounting net asset backing per ordinary share.

Trace statement inputs:

Ordinary shareholders’ equity divided by ordinary shares outstanding.

Free cash flow

Cash flow from operating activities − Capital expenditure needed to maintain capacity

Cash available after maintaining operating asset base; interpretive, not a basic journal entry.

Trace source:

Use operating cash flow from the Statement of Cash Flows and capital expenditure from investing activities or PPE additions. This is a cash-flow interpretation measure, not an SPL or SFP only figure.

Practice case-data route

For integrated practice questions, read the source data in this order before doing any entry:

1. Trial balance extract

Identify existing balances and whether each is debit or credit before adjustment.

2. Additional information

Number each item and decide whether it affects SPL, SFP, both, or no entry.

3. Calculation bridge

Show shortage, NRV, accrual, prepayment, allowance, tax or finance-cost workings before journals.

4. Journal and ledger effect

Prepare the General Journal, then confirm the adjusted balance agrees to the statement presentation.

Explanation layer for practice answers

When revising practice pages, read the explanation under each model answer after checking the numbers. It tells you why the treatment is used, not merely what the debit and credit are.

Use it to train exam language: “because the asset is controlled”, “because the liability is redeemable”, “because the bank has recorded it but the books have not”, or “because the allowance method separates estimate, write-off and recovery”.

Cash-flow classification route

Before classifying cash-flow items, separate profit effects from cash movements. Sale of PPE: gain or loss is SPL; proceeds are investing. Depreciation: non-cash add-back. Share issue, borrowing, loan repayment and dividends paid: financing route unless the question states a different rule.
