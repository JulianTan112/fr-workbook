Exam prep — synced with Lessons + Practice

This section is now a route map for exam answering. It does not replace the practice workspaces; it tells learners which accounting format to use, which traps to check, and which full worked practice to revisit.

1. Format-first answering route

Read requirementJournal? Ledger? Statement? Reconciliation?
Extract dataDate, amount, account, term, GST, timing
Choose formatJournal / T-account / schedule / statement
Compute bridgeShow formulas and subtotals
Post / presentUse proper accounting layout
Self-checkTotals balance and treatment logic matches lesson

Accounting answer standard: Use the format requested by the question. If it asks for journal entries, ledgers, schedules, reconciliation statements or financial statements, present the answer in that accounting layout first; explanations should support the layout, not replace it.

2. High-risk exam traps by lesson

AreaTrap check
Regulatory / EthicsName the threat exactly: self-interest, self-review, advocacy, familiarity or intimidation. Then identify why judgement/objectivity is at risk.
Accounting EquationCollection from a credit customer is not new revenue. It is Dr Cash / Cr Accounts Receivable only.
Recording TransactionsUse AED LRC first. Then journalise, post to ledger, prepare trial balance, then statements.
Trading BusinessUnder perpetual inventory, sales of goods usually need two entries: revenue side and cost side.
FCA / CPTGoods shipped before year-end are not automatically buyer inventory. Check ownership transfer: FCA origin/handed to carrier vs CPT destination/arrival.
Adjusting EntriesStandard balance-day adjustments generally do not use Cash at Bank.
InventoryPhysical possession is not ownership. Consignment goods remain inventory of consignor until third-party sale.
Bank ReconciliationDeposits-in-transit and unpresented cheques are bank-side timing items; bank charges, EFT receipts and dishonoured cheques are book-side journal items.
ReceivablesAllowance write-off does not create a new impairment expense; Dr Allowance / Cr A/R.
PPEAccumulated depreciation is a contra-asset, not a liability.
GST disposalGST is calculated once on selling price excluding GST. Gain is sale proceeds excluding GST less carrying amount.
EquityDividends declared are distributions from retained earnings and create dividends payable; they are not expenses.
LiabilitiesClassify current vs non-current by settlement timing, not by account name.
Company SPL/SFPPlace gain on disposal under other income; interest under financial expenses; auditors’ and directors’ remuneration under administrative expenses.

6. Term traps that change the answer format

Term trapWhat the examiner is testingSafe answer move
EBIT vs Profit before taxWhether interest/finance expense has already been deducted.EBIT excludes interest and tax; PBT excludes tax only.
Operating profit vs Profit after taxWhether the learner deducts tax too early.Operating profit comes before finance expenses and tax in the course company SPL logic.
Taxable income vs accounting profitWhether tax is calculated on the correct base.Use taxable income when provided. Do not assume it equals accounting PBT unless question says so.
Impairment expense vs allowanceSPL vs SFP separation.Expense/loss goes SPL; allowance/contra account deducts the asset in SFP.
Contra account vs liabilityClassification and presentation.Allowance and accumulated depreciation are deductions from assets, not external liabilities.
