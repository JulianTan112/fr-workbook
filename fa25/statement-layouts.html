Statement reference page

SFP, SPL and worksheet layout references

Use this page as the visual anchor before solving formula, adjustment and statement-preparation questions. The layouts are clean teaching redraws based on the attached statement examples: they show where each item normally lives without copying the scanned pages.

Fast map: what each layout is for

SFP or balance sheet

Resources and claims at a date

Shows assets, liabilities and shareholders’ equity at the reporting date. Its anchor is Assets = Liabilities + Shareholders’ Equity.

SPL or statement of operations

Performance for a period

Shows revenue, operating expenses, other income or expenses, tax and profit. It explains how the period’s profit was earned.

Worksheet

Bridge from records to statements

Starts with the unadjusted trial balance, applies adjusting entries, produces the adjusted trial balance, then splits balances into SPL and SFP columns.

1. Statement of Financial Position or Balance Sheet layout

Read the SFP vertically: assets first, then liabilities, then equity. The right side or lower section explains who has claims against the assets: creditors first, then owners.

How to use it: cash, receivables, inventory and prepayments sit under current assets. PPE sits under non-current assets. Accounts payable, accrued expenses and deferred revenue usually sit under liabilities. Share capital and retained earnings sit under equity.

Self-check: total assets must equal total liabilities plus shareholders’ equity. If they do not agree, the statement is not balanced.

Administrative expenses: they belong in the SPL under operating expenses. They are not SFP assets and they are not selling expenses.

Cost of sales: use this as the formal SPL presentation line where the question gives a trading company layout. Do not automatically rename it as cost of goods sold. Cost of goods sold is a supporting inventory or trading-schedule working.

How to use it: assets, contra-assets, liabilities and equity go to the SFP columns. Revenue and expenses go to the SPL columns. Net income is the balancing amount that connects the two statements.

Common mistake: the worksheet is not the final published statement. It is the bridge that proves the adjusted trial balance can be separated into the SPL and SFP.
