Explanation for FCA/CPT goods-in-transit cutoff

Due-diligence treatment check added against the relevant financial-accounting principle.

The answer turns on control/ownership at year-end, not physical location alone. For FCA-style facts, the buyer normally includes goods once the seller hands them to the carrier at the named place. For CPT destination-style facts used in this workbook, the buyer does not include goods before arrival at destination if the question makes destination control decisive.

Inventory inclusion drives both SFP inventory and related payable/cash recognition. Freight-in is included in inventory cost when the buyer bears cost necessary to bring inventory to its present location and condition.

Due-diligence check: write “who owns the goods at year-end?” before deciding whether to include inventory.
