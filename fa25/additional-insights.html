Additional insights — Financial Accounting strengthened by source audit

This page separates what belongs in Financial Accounting treatment from what belongs in interpretation. It uses the two Financial Accounting references as the core source map, and uses Financial Management ratios only where they help learners interpret accounting statements.

Symbol convention: all division in formulas uses the forward slash /, not the old division glyph, so it is visually distinct from the plus sign +. Example: Gross profit / Sales revenue × 100.

Formula bank — highlighted and usable

This is the corrected formula bank. It expands abbreviations such as EPS and ROCE, separates formula text from explanation text, and shows exactly where learners obtain each figure.

L04 or L06

Gross profit

FormulaSales revenue − Cost of sales
Trace statement inputs:

Sales revenue − cost of sales = gross profit.

How to read it:

Use after adjusting purchases, returns, freight-in, closing inventory, shortage or surplus and obsolescence.

L04, L06 or L10

Gross profit margin

FormulaGross profit / Sales revenue × 100
Trace statement inputs:

Gross profit divided by sales revenue. Gross profit is sales revenue less cost of sales.

How to read it:

Shows how much sales remains after inventory cost. Do not deduct admin, selling or finance costs here.

L10

Operating profit

FormulaGross profit + Other income − Selling expenses − Administrative expenses
Trace statement inputs:

Gross profit + other income − selling or distribution expenses − administrative expenses or G&A = operating profit or EBIT area.

How to read it:

Profit generated from operations before finance costs and tax in common layouts.

L10

Operating profit margin

FormulaOperating profit / Sales revenue × 100
Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit divided by sales revenue.

How to read it:

Measures operating return on sales after operating expenses.

L09 or L10

ROCE — Return on capital employed

FormulaOperating profit / Long-term capital employed × 100
Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit divided by capital employed. Capital employed is normally equity plus non-current liabilities.

How to read it:

Shows return generated on long-term funds. It combines profitability and efficiency.

L10

Sales revenue to capital employed

FormulaSales revenue / Capital employed
Trace statement inputs:

Sales revenue divided by capital employed. Capital employed normally comes from equity plus non-current liabilities.

How to read it:

Explains how efficiently long-term funds generate sales.

L10

Current ratio

FormulaCurrent assets / Current liabilities
Trace statement inputs:

Current assets divided by current liabilities.

How to read it:

Tests short-term liquidity. A high ratio is not automatically good if assets are idle.

L07 or L10

Acid-test or quick ratio

Formula(Current assets − Inventory) / Current liabilities
Trace statement inputs:

Quick assets divided by current liabilities. Quick assets exclude inventory.

How to read it:

Stricter liquidity test because inventory may not turn quickly into cash.

L07

Receivables turnover

FormulaCredit sales / Average trade receivables
Trace source:

Use credit sales from sales records or SPL sales if all sales are credit, and average trade receivables from SFP trade receivables.

How to read it:

Measures how often receivables are converted into cash during the year.

L07

Receivables collection period

FormulaAverage trade receivables / Credit sales × 365
Trace source:

Use average trade receivables from SFP trade receivables and credit sales from sales records or SPL sales if all sales are credit.

How to read it:

Measures average days customers take to pay.

L06

Inventory turnover

FormulaCost of goods sold / Average inventory
Trace statement inputs:

Use cost of goods sold from the inventory or trading schedule and average inventory. Average inventory normally uses opening and closing inventory.

How to read it:

Measures how many times average inventory is sold in the year.

L06

Inventory turnover period

FormulaAverage inventory / Cost of goods sold × 365
Trace statement inputs:

Use cost of goods sold from the inventory or trading schedule and average inventory. Average inventory normally uses opening and closing inventory.

How to read it:

Measures average days inventory is held before sale.

L07 or L10

Payables settlement period

FormulaAverage trade payables / Credit purchases × 365
Trace source:

Use average trade payables from the SFP and credit purchases from purchase records. If credit purchases are not given, use the amount specified by the question.

How to read it:

Measures average days taken to pay suppliers.

L07 or L10

Operating cash cycle

FormulaInventory days + Receivables days − Payables days
Trace source:

This combines three separate working-capital timing measures: inventory days + receivable days − payable days. The components are built from SPL or SFP inputs and credit records.

How to read it:

Shows how long cash is tied up in inventory and receivables before supplier credit offsets it.

L10

Gearing ratio

FormulaDebt / (Share capital + Reserves + Debt) × 100
Trace statement inputs:

Debt or borrowings divided by (share capital + reserves or retained earnings + debt).

How to read it:

Measures how much long-term capital comes from lenders rather than owners.

L10

Interest cover

FormulaOperating profit / Interest expense
Trace statement inputs:

Operating profit divided by interest expense or finance cost.

How to read it:

Shows how many times current operating profit covers interest cost.

L09

EPS — Earnings per share

Formula(Profit after tax − Preference dividends) / Weighted-average ordinary shares
Trace source:

Profit after tax minus preference dividends from equity or dividend records, divided by weighted-average ordinary shares from the share records. Market price is not used in EPS.

How to read it:

Shows profit attributable to each ordinary share.

L09

Dividend payout ratio

FormulaOrdinary dividends / Earnings available to ordinary shareholders × 100
Trace source:

Ordinary dividends from dividend or equity records or earnings available to ordinary shareholders from SPL profit after tax adjusted for preference dividends.

How to read it:

Shows how much ordinary shareholder profit is distributed.

L09

Dividend yield

FormulaDividend per share / Market price per share × 100
Trace source:

Dividend per share comes from dividend records; market price per share is outside the financial statements. Do not look for market price inside the SPL or SFP.

How to read it:

Investor cash return based on current share price.

L09

Price-earnings ratio

FormulaMarket price per share / EPS
Trace source:

Market price per share is outside the financial statements. EPS is calculated from profit after tax, preference dividends and ordinary shares.

How to read it:

Shows how many times annual earnings investors are paying for one share.

L09

Book value per share

FormulaOrdinary shareholders’ equity / Ordinary shares outstanding
Trace statement inputs:

Ordinary shareholders’ equity divided by ordinary shares outstanding.

How to read it:

Accounting net asset backing per ordinary share; not market value.

Additional insights

Free cash flow

FormulaCash flow from operating activities − Capital expenditure
Trace source:

Use cash flow from operating activities and capital expenditure from investing activities or PPE additions. This is a cash-flow interpretation measure, not an SPL or SFP only figure. Open full cash-flow activity guide →

How to read it:

Cash remaining after maintaining or expanding asset base; interpretive, not a journal entry.

Lesson-by-lesson additional insight map

Lesson map

Lesson 01 · Regulatory Environment

  • IFRS framework lens — Use the “Why or Who or What or How” sequence: objective → users → useful qualities → recognition, measurement, presentation and disclosure.
  • Cost constraint — More disclosure is not automatically better. Information should be useful enough to justify the cost of preparing and verifying it.
  • Financial accounting vs bookkeeping — Bookkeeping records transactions; financial accounting selects, measures, presents and communicates information to external users.
Lesson map

Lesson 02 · Accounting Equation & Financial Statements

  • Two simple equations — SFP equation: Assets = Liabilities + Equity. Performance equation: Profit = Income − Expenses.
  • Statement relationship — SPL measures performance; SCE explains owner or equity movements; SFP reports position at date; cash-flow thinking explains cash generation.
  • Entity assumption — Owner and business are accounted for separately; owner drawings are not expenses.
Lesson map

Lesson 03 · Recording Business Transactions

  • Normal balances — Assets, expenses and drawings normally increase on debit; liabilities, revenue and capital or share capital normally increase on credit.
  • Posting logic — The journal gives chronology; the ledger gives account-by-account accumulation.
  • Error awareness — Equal debit and credit totals can still hide omissions, wrong accounts, wrong amounts on both sides or compensating errors.
Lesson map

Lesson 04 · Trading Businesses

  • Perpetual system — Record inventory movement continuously: sale entry plus cost entry.
  • Gross profit analysis — Gross profit = Sales revenue − Cost of sales. Gross profit margin explains how much of each sales dollar remains after inventory cost.
  • Operating cost separation — Freight-in is inventory cost for buyer; freight-out or delivery is selling expense for seller.
Lesson map

Lesson 05 · Balance Day Adjustments

  • Recognition trigger — Ask: has the benefit been consumed, revenue earned, obligation incurred, or asset used?
  • Adjusted trial balance — Statements must use adjusted balances, not raw unadjusted balances.
  • Depreciation is allocation — Depreciation allocates PPE cost over useful life; it is not a cash payment at adjustment date.
Lesson map

Lesson 06 · Inventories

  • Basic relationship — Opening inventory + Purchases − Ending inventory = Cost of goods sold, adjusted for returns/freight/shortage as relevant.
  • Inventory error effect — Ending inventory overstated → Cost of goods sold understated → profit overstated → equity overstated.
  • Efficiency link — Inventory turnover period connects accounting records to working-capital management.
Lesson map

Lesson 07 · Cash, Receivables & Bank Reconciliation

  • Cash collection formula — Cash collected from customers can be estimated from sales and movement in accounts receivable.
  • Allowance logic — Estimate impairment first; later write-off usually uses allowance, not a fresh expense.
  • Working capital link — Receivable days, inventory days and payable days explain the cash operating cycle.
Lesson map

Lesson 08 · Property, Plant & Equipment

  • Initial measurement — PPE cost includes purchase price and directly attributable costs needed to bring the asset to location and condition for intended use.
  • Subsequent expenditure — Capitalise only when future economic benefits are enhanced beyond maintaining current condition.
  • Asset performance link — Asset turnover and ROCE connect PPE balances to efficiency and profitability analysis.
Lesson map

Lesson 09 · Shareholders’ Equity

  • Ordinary vs preference shares — Preference shares may carry dividend priority; redeemable preference shares can create liability-style obligations depending on terms.
  • Retained earnings bridge — Opening retained earnings + profit − dividends = closing retained earnings.
  • Market interpretation — Price-earnings and dividend yield use market price, so they are interpretation tools rather than journal entries.
Lesson map

Lesson 10 · Current & Non-Current Liabilities

  • Tax distinction — Tax expense appears in SPL; tax payable appears in SFP until settled.
  • GST distinction — Output GST is collected on behalf of IRAS; input GST is recoverable when claimable; revenue or gain excludes GST.
  • Solvency interpretation — Interest cover = Operating profit / Interest payable. Low cover signals pressure to meet finance costs.
