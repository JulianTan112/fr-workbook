1. Role of accounting in business

Accounting identifies, records and communicates an organisation’s business activities. It provides financial data through financial statements; finance uses financial capital information for decision-making; economics provides broader context about economic environments.

Types of businesses
Business typeWhat it doesAccounting focus
Service businessProvides services for fees.Service revenue, receivables, expenses.
Trading businessBuys finished goods and sells them to customers.Inventory, sales, COGS, gross profit.
Manufacturing businessConverts raw materials into finished goods.Raw materials, work-in-progress, finished goods, production cost.

5. Accounting principles and assumptions

Principles and assumptions
Principle / assumptionMeaningExam phrase
Business entity assumptionThe business is accounted for separately from the owner(s).Owner drawings are not business expenses.
Going concern assumptionFinancial statements assume the business will continue operating in the foreseeable future.Assets are not automatically measured at liquidation value.
Cost principle / historical costInformation is based on actual costs incurred in transactions.Record equipment at cost paid, not estimated resale value.
Accrual concept / revenue recognitionRevenue is recorded when goods/services are delivered, regardless of cash receipt.Service on credit creates Accounts Receivable and Revenue.
Matching principleExpenses incurred to earn revenue are matched against that revenue in the same period.Wages, COGS and depreciation are recorded in the correct period.
Accounting period principleBusiness activity is divided into reporting periods.Monthly/annual financial statements require cut-off.

6. Accounting equation and elements

The accounting equation is the spine of the statement of financial position:

Assets = Liabilities + Equity. Equity increases from capital contributions and profit; equity decreases from drawings/withdrawals and losses.
Accounting elements
ElementDefinition / roleExamples
AssetsResources controlled by the business with future economic benefits.Cash, accounts receivable, supplies, inventory, equipment, land.
LiabilitiesPresent obligations to transfer economic resources because of past events.Accounts payable, bank loan, salaries payable, tax payable.
EquityResidual interest after deducting liabilities from assets.Capital, retained earnings, share capital.
RevenueIncome from ordinary activities; increases equity through profit.Service revenue, sales revenue, fees earned.
ExpensesCosts incurred to earn revenue; reduce equity through profit/loss.Wages, rent, utilities, COGS.
DrawingsOwner withdrawals; reduce equity but are not expenses.Cash or supplies taken for personal use.
