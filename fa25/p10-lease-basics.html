IFRS 16 lease basics — right-of-use asset and lease liability

Open liabilities lesson →

1. Lease filter: is there a lease?

QuestionPass conditionFail condition
Is there an identified asset?The contract specifies the asset, and the supplier cannot freely substitute it throughout the use period.The supplier can substitute the asset and benefits from doing so.
Does the customer obtain substantially all economic benefits from use?The customer receives most of the output, capacity, access or use benefits.Other parties continue to obtain significant use benefits.
Does the customer direct the use?The customer decides how and for what purpose the asset is used, or has the relevant operating rights when decisions are predetermined.The supplier controls how and for what purpose the asset is used.
Lease exists only when all three are present:
Identified asset + economic benefits + right to direct use

2. Initial recognition

DateAccount or ParticularsDebit ($)Credit ($)

The lease liability starts at the present value of lease payments not paid at commencement. The right-of-use asset usually starts with the lease liability and adds relevant initial direct costs.

4. Current or non-current split

For arrears payments, after calculating the lease liability schedule, the non-current portion is the balance still outstanding after the next 12 months. The current portion is the difference between the total closing liability and the non-current portion.

Total lease liability at reporting date
− Non-current portion after next 12 months
= Current lease liability
Self-check: A lease creates both an asset and a liability. The asset is used up through depreciation; the liability is unwound through interest and cash payments.

5. Service contract trap

A contract can involve a specified asset but still fail lease accounting if the customer does not direct how and for what purpose the asset is used. In that case, the customer may be buying a service rather than leasing the asset.
