Explanation for lower of cost and NRV answer

Due-diligence treatment check added against the relevant financial-accounting principle.

Lower of cost and NRV applies prudence: inventory should not be carried above the amount expected to be recovered from sale. NRV is estimated selling price less costs to complete and costs necessary to sell.

When NRV is below cost, the loss is recognised immediately through COGS or an inventory write-down/obsolescence expense, depending on the required presentation. The SFP then carries inventory at the lower recoverable amount.

Due-diligence check: calculate NRV first, compare item-by-item or category as required by the question, then record only the excess of cost over NRV as the write-down.
