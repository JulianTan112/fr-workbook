1. Asset and PPE definition

Asset and PPE definition
ConceptDefinition / treatment
AssetA present economic resource controlled by an entity as a result of past events, with potential to produce future economic benefits.
Property, plant and equipmentTangible items with physical substance, held for production/supply of goods or services, rental to others or administrative use, and expected to be used for more than one accounting period.
Not PPEItems held primarily for resale are inventory, not PPE.

2. Capital vs revenue expenditure

Capital vs revenue expenditure
FeatureCapital expenditureRevenue expenditure
Benefit periodMore than one accounting period.Current period or short-term benefit.
PurposeAcquire/improve long-term asset or bring asset to working condition.Maintain day-to-day operations.
Accounting entryDr PPE / Non-current Asset; Cr Cash/Payable.Dr Expense; Cr Cash/Payable.
Financial statement effectSFP asset first, then depreciation over useful life.SPL expense immediately.
ExamplesMachine purchase, delivery, installation, testing, major upgrade.Repairs, regular maintenance, annual insurance, staff training.

5. Depreciation

Depreciation allocates depreciable amount over useful life. It is not a cash payment and it is not a market valuation exercise.

Depreciation terms
TermMeaning
Depreciable amountCost less residual value.
Useful lifePeriod or units over which asset is expected to be used.
Residual valueEstimated amount recoverable at end of useful life.
Accumulated depreciationContra-asset account that accumulates depreciation charged to date.
Carrying amount / NBVCost less accumulated depreciation and impairment.
Depreciation journal entry
DateAccount / ExplanationDebit ($)Credit ($)
Period endDepreciation ExpenseX
   Accumulated DepreciationX
Record depreciation for the period.
Depreciation methods
MethodFormulaUse pattern
Straight-line(Cost − Residual Value) ÷ Useful LifeEqual expense each period.
Double-declining balanceNet book value × double straight-line rateHigher expense earlier; ensure not below residual value.
Units-of-productionDepreciable amount × actual units / total expected unitsBased on asset usage; noted as not covered in some materials.

7. Disposal of PPE

On disposal, depreciate up to disposal date, remove the asset cost, remove accumulated depreciation, record proceeds, and recognise gain or loss.

Disposal steps
StepAccounting treatment
1. Update depreciationRecord depreciation to disposal date if required.
2. Remove accumulated depreciationDr Accumulated Depreciation.
3. Remove asset costCr PPE asset cost.
4. Record proceedsDr Cash / Receivable.
5. Balance gain/lossCredit gain if proceeds exceed carrying amount; debit loss if proceeds are below carrying amount.

8. Contra accounts and impairment in PPE

A contra account has the opposite normal balance from the main account and is shown as a deduction from that main account. This lets the statement show both original cost and the reduction accumulated over time.

PPE contra-account presentation
Main accountContra accountNormal balanceSFP effect
Delivery vehicles / Office equipment / MachineryAccumulated DepreciationCreditDeducted from PPE cost to show carrying amount.
PPE costAccumulated Impairment Loss, if usedCreditDeducted when recoverable amount is below carrying amount.
Carrying amount = Cost − Accumulated depreciation − Accumulated impairment loss, if any

Exam trap: accumulated depreciation is not a liability. It is a contra-asset. It reduces asset carrying amount, but it does not represent an amount owed to outsiders.
