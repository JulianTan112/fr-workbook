🏭 Property, Plant & Equipment

Capitalise long-term tangible assets, allocate cost through depreciation, and account for subsequent expenditure and disposal.

Why It Matters — learning outcome bridge

These points explain why the learning outcomes are not isolated textbook headings. They show what the learner should be able to do in a practice or exam setting.

Capitalising vs expensing changes profit timing

Capital expenditure creates an asset and is expensed over time; revenue expenditure hits profit immediately.

Carrying amount is not market value

Cost less accumulated depreciation and impairment is an accounting carrying amount.

Disposal combines asset removal and gain or loss

Correct disposal requires removing cost, removing accumulated depreciation, recording consideration and balancing gain or loss.

Applied explanation bridge — PPE as investing activity and carrying amount

PPE answers must show three layers: initial cost, subsequent depreciation and disposal. The cash-flow treatment is separate from the SPL gain or loss.

AcquisitionPurchase price and directly attributable costs needed to bring the asset to intended use.Capitalise eligible costs as PPE; exclude period costs after the asset is ready for use.SFP non-current asset increases; cash or payable decreases or increases.
DepreciationCost, residual value, useful life, usage period or given depreciation amount.Allocate cost over useful life under going concern and accrual basis.SPL depreciation expense; SFP accumulated depreciation increases.
DisposalCost, accumulated depreciation, proceeds and disposal date.Carrying amount = cost − accumulated depreciation; compare proceeds with carrying amount.SPL gain or loss; SFP removes PPE and accumulated depreciation.

Formula focus — PPE

L08

Initial PPE cost

FormulaPPE cost = Purchase price + directly attributable costs − discounts or rebates
Trace source:

Use purchase invoice plus directly attributable costs from the question. The capitalised total appears as PPE in non-current assets, not as an immediate SPL expense.

How to read it:

Capitalise costs needed to bring the asset to location and condition for use.

L08

Carrying amount

FormulaCarrying amount = Cost − Accumulated depreciation − Accumulated impairment loss
Trace source:

Use PPE cost less accumulated depreciation and any impairment deduction shown in the SFP.

How to read it:

Carrying amount is the net book amount, not original cost or selling price.

L08

Gain or loss on disposal

FormulaGain or loss = Disposal proceeds excluding GST − Carrying amount
Trace source:

Use disposal proceeds excluding GST from the disposal information, then compare against carrying amount from PPE less accumulated depreciation. Gain appears under other income or gain on disposal; loss appears in SPL expenses.

How to read it:

GST is output tax collected for authority; it is not part of the gain.

5. Depreciation

Depreciation allocates depreciable amount over useful life. It is not a cash payment and it is not a market valuation exercise.

Depreciation terms
TermMeaning
Depreciable amountCost less residual value.
Useful lifePeriod or units over which asset is expected to be used.
Residual valueEstimated amount recoverable at end of useful life.
Accumulated depreciationContra-asset account that accumulates depreciation charged to date.
Carrying amount or NBVCost less accumulated depreciation and impairment.

7. Disposal of PPE

On disposal, depreciate up to disposal date, remove the asset cost, remove accumulated depreciation, record proceeds, and recognise gain or loss.

Disposal steps
StepAccounting treatment
1. Update depreciationRecord depreciation to disposal date if required.
2. Remove accumulated depreciationDr Accumulated Depreciation.
3. Remove asset costCr PPE asset cost.
4. Record proceedsDr Cash or Receivable.
5. Balance gain or lossCredit gain if proceeds exceed carrying amount; debit loss if proceeds are below carrying amount.

Gain = proceeds 1,500 − carrying amount 1,250 = 250

Boundary check: The comparison is proceeds against carrying amount, not proceeds against original cost.

8. Contra accounts and impairment in PPE

A contra account has the opposite normal balance from the main account and is shown as a deduction from that main account. This lets the statement show both original cost and the reduction accumulated over time.

PPE contra-account presentation
Main accountContra accountNormal balanceSFP effect
Delivery vehicles or Office equipment or MachineryAccumulated DepreciationCreditDeducted from PPE cost to show carrying amount.
PPE costAccumulated Impairment Loss, if usedCreditDeducted when recoverable amount is below carrying amount.
Carrying amount = Cost − Accumulated depreciation − Accumulated impairment loss, if any

Exam trap: accumulated depreciation is not a liability. It is a contra-asset. It reduces asset carrying amount, but it does not represent an amount owed to outsiders.

PPE disposal and cash-flow classification

PPE disposal produces two separate effects: the gain or loss goes to SPL, but the cash proceeds from selling PPE belong in investing activities. Under the indirect method, deduct a disposal gain or add back a disposal loss because the proceeds are reported separately under investing activities.
