1. Company structure

A company is a legal entity formed under law. Owners are shareholders. A share represents a fraction of ownership. Companies with share capital may be private or public, and the structure affects fundraising, ownership and regulatory requirements.

Types of companies
Company typeKey feature
Private company limited by shares1 to 50 shareholders; restrictions on share transfer; “Pte Ltd”.
Exempt private companyNot more than 20 shareholders and no corporate shareholder; may have audit exemptions.
Public company limited by sharesMore than 50 shareholders; may offer shares/debentures to public; “Ltd”.
Public company limited by guaranteeNo share capital; members guarantee amount on winding up; often non-profit/public-interest activities.

2. Share capital: ordinary and preference shares

Share types
InstrumentMain featureEquity/liability issue
Ordinary sharesVoting rights and residual claim to dividends/assets after preference rights.Equity.
Non-redeemable preference sharesPriority dividend features but no mandatory redemption.Generally equity in this course treatment.
Redeemable preference sharesRedemption obligation at a future date.May be treated as liability when redemption is mandatory, depending on terms.

4. Reserves and retained earnings

Equity components
Equity componentMeaning
Share capitalAmounts contributed by shareholders through share issues.
Retained earningsAccumulated profits retained in the business after dividends.
Revenue reserveReserve arising from profits that may generally be available for distribution depending on law/policy.
Capital reserveReserve arising from capital transactions; generally not ordinary operating profit.

5. Dividends

Dividends are distributions to shareholders, not expenses. A liability arises when dividends are declared and payable.

Dividend entries
DateAccount / ExplanationDebit ($)Credit ($)
DeclarationRetained EarningsX
   Dividends PayableX
Dividend declared before year-end and payable later.
PaymentDividends PayableX
   Cash at BankX
Pay declared dividend.
Dividend calculation
Dividend typeCalculation focus
Ordinary dividendNumber of ordinary shares × dividend rate declared.
Preference dividendPreference shares × stipulated dividend rate; consider interim dividends already paid.
Redeemable preference returnMay be treated as finance cost/dividend payable according to classification and course convention.
